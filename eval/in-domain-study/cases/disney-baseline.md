# Disney (DIS): Equity Research Note — June 2026

**Recommendation: BUY** (12-month target ~$128, ~27% upside from ~$101)

## The Setup

Disney trades at roughly **$101**, near the bottom of its 52-week range ($92–$125), down ~5% YTD and off ~14% from recent highs. Market cap is ~$175B. This is a fallen large-cap that has decoupled from improving fundamentals — and that gap is the opportunity.

The stock carries a trailing P/E of ~16x, but the more relevant figure is the **forward P/E of ~13–15x against guided ~12% EPS growth**. That is a sub-1.0 PEG for a company with a globally dominant IP library, a parks business with structural pricing power, and a streaming business that just crossed into double-digit margins. The five-year average forward multiple is ~23x; even a partial re-rating toward ~17–18x on rising earnings gets you to the high-$120s/low-$130s.

## What's Working (the bull case)

**1. Streaming inflected from drag to engine.** Fiscal Q2 2026 was the pivot quarter: entertainment SVOD (Disney+/Hulu) revenue grew 13% to $5.49B and operating income jumped **88% to $582M**, hitting a **10.6% operating margin** — the first time the segment broke double digits. Management reaffirmed ≥10% margin for full-year FY26. The bear thesis of the last five years — "streaming will never make money" — is now empirically dead. Each incremental margin point on a $20B+ revenue base is meaningful operating leverage, and Disney is still early on price increases, password-sharing crackdown, and ad-tier monetization.

**2. Earnings momentum and beats.** Q2 revenue rose 7% to $25.2B; adjusted EPS rose 8% to $1.57, beating the $1.49 consensus. Guidance is for **~12% adjusted EPS growth** in FY26 (16% including the 53rd week), with Q3 segment operating income guided to ~$5.3B. This is a company raising, not cutting.

**3. Capital return.** At least **$8B in buybacks** planned for FY26 — meaningful at a $175B cap (~4.5% of shares) and especially accretive while the stock is depressed. Plus a ~1.5% dividend. Management is signaling that the stock is cheap by aggressively shrinking the float.

**4. Succession overhang cleared.** Josh D'Amaro became CEO on March 18, ending the multi-year Iger-succession soap opera that had been a persistent discount on the multiple. D'Amaro ran Experiences (the crown-jewel parks segment) and is well-regarded operationally. A known, credible operator removes a discrete uncertainty.

**5. Parks remain the cash machine.** Domestic park demand is described as "healthy," and a multi-year capital expansion program is underway. This segment funds the entire transition and has demonstrated repeated pricing power through cycles.

## What Could Break It (the bear case)

**1. Consumer/macro sensitivity in Parks.** Experiences is the profit anchor, and management explicitly flagged "macroeconomic uncertainty consumers are facing." A late-2026 recession would hit discretionary travel and park spend hardest — this is the single biggest cyclical risk and the reason for a Buy rather than a table-pounding conviction call.

**2. Linear TV secular decline.** The cable bundle is still bleeding, and it remains a large cash-flow contributor. Streaming growth has to outrun linear erosion in absolute dollars, not just optically. This is a race, and the spread is narrower than the streaming headlines suggest.

**3. ESPN DTC execution.** The ESPN direct-to-consumer transition is the next big bet. If the standalone product doesn't scale fast enough to offset linear sports-affiliate revenue loss, a revenue air-pocket opens. Execution risk is real but the strategic direction is correct — sports is the stickiest live content.

**4. Subscriber opacity.** Disney no longer discloses Disney+ subscriber counts, forcing reliance on revenue/margin proxies. Less transparency means the market can't easily verify the growth story, which can suppress the multiple until profitability is undeniable.

## The Call

The asymmetry favors buyers. You're paying ~13–15x forward earnings for **double-digit EPS growth, an inflecting streaming business, a buyback equal to ~4.5% of the cap, a resolved leadership question, and a sentiment trough**. Downside is reasonably protected by the parks/IP asset value and the trough multiple; the main risk (consumer cyclicality) is macro, not company-specific deterioration. Analyst consensus is "Buy/Strong Buy" with a ~$129 average target — and unlike many consensus longs, the entry price here is genuinely depressed rather than extended.

**BUY.** Best suited to investors with a 12–24 month horizon who can tolerate macro-driven volatility. Trim the thesis if domestic park attendance/per-cap turns negative or if streaming margin progress stalls below the 10% FY26 commitment — those are the two metrics that would convert this from Buy to Hold.
