# Intel (INTC): Equity Research Call — HOLD (no fresh capital; "right-but-priced / right-but-unforecastable")

**Procedure followed:** the `research-and-pick-a-stock` playbook spine S1→S11. The call is gated, not narrative — it is determined principally at **S3** (critical-factor filter), **S6** (scenario debiasing), and **S7/S8** (four-part edge predicate + catalyst/convergence). The methods that actually moved the verdict were the *four-part edge predicate*, the *conjunctive materiality-plus-catalyst filter*, *adjust-the-consensus-baseline*, *bet-on-people-where-no-moat-exists*, and *require-time-to-convergence*.

## S1 — Attention frontier
The only place a differentiated view on Intel can live is the foundry (IFS) turnaround: specifically whether **18A/14A yields ramp to economics that win external customers**. Everything else (PC/client share, DCAI vs. AMD, the Mobileye/Altera stubs) is well-covered, slow-moving, and already in the tape. If I can't name where my divergence comes from, it's coverage, not a call. **Divergence frontier = process technology execution + external-foundry commitment.** Everything else is parked.

## S3 — Critical factors (conjunctive filter: material AND forecastable-by-me AND not-yet-priced)
- **18A/14A yield ramp** — material (it *is* the equity), but **the trigger is not forecastable by my own effort**. Yield curves inside a fab are the single most information-asymmetric variable in semis; the firm itself routinely misjudges them. Per the *materiality-plus-catalyst filter*, a material driver whose trigger I cannot independently forecast is a **risk to monitor, not a critical factor to research** (S3 gate). It fails.
- **External foundry wins (a marquee logic customer at scale)** — material, forecastable-ish via channel checks, and **partly unpriced**. This is the one survivor that could carry an edge.
- **Government/CHIPS + equity stake dynamics, capex cadence** — material but **exogenous and largely priced** (politically telegraphed). Monitor, don't research.
- **PC/server share** — forecastable but **not material enough to re-rate** and largely consensus.

Culled set (top 2–4): external-foundry traction, and capital intensity vs. free-cash-flow burn. That's the entire researchable surface.

## S4 — Sourcing the survivor
The honest read on "external foundry wins": after years, the order book remains **packaging/advanced-packaging and internal product, not the marquee, high-volume leading-edge logic customer** that would prove IFS as a TSMC alternative. *Adjust-the-consensus-baseline:* the bullish "18A is on track" narrative rests on a **thin, optimism-biased, company-sourced** baseline (management guidance + sell-side echo), not independent corroboration. I refuse to treat divergence from that as edge until a structurally independent corroborator (a named, volume customer taping out on 18A/14A) appears. None has, at scale.

## S5/S6 — Forecast + forced dual-tail scenarios (debiasing)
Moving only the two highest-leverage drivers (foundry traction × FCF burn), bounding tails to history:
- **Upside (~25%):** 18A yields hold, a top-tier external customer commits, IFS turns contribution-positive ~2027–28. The stock is a multi-bagger off a depressed base.
- **Base (~50%):** Yields are "fine," product stabilizes, but IFS stays sub-scale and cash-hungry; Intel is a low-return, capital-intensive IDM trading on optionality. Stock roughly range-bound, fairly valued on P/B and out-year EPS that won't compound.
- **Downside (~25%):** A yield slip or capex cut signals 18A can't win external logic; IFS gets curtailed/structurally separated at a loss. Material drawdown.

The S6 gate forces an honest downside — and here the downside is genuinely uncomfortable, while the base case is *not* the hoped-for case. Good: the debias held.

## S7 — Valuation + the four-part edge predicate (the central gate)
Value on the **audience's actual method**: Intel does not earn a clean P/E here, so the market prices it on **price-to-book / sum-of-the-parts and out-year EPS optionality** — which already embeds a meaningful "turnaround call option." That is the problem. Running the **four-part edge predicate** on the bull case:
1. **Material?** Yes.
2. **In-horizon (12–18mo)?** Marginal — proof of IFS economics is a 2027–28 event.
3. **Forecastable by me?** **No** (yield ramp; see S3).
4. **Not yet priced?** **No** — the stock already trades as an embedded option on the turnaround; the optimism is in the multiple.

It **fails clauses 3 and 4** — the two most decisive. By the S7 hard stop: *right-but-priced, and mispriced-but-unforecastable → no trade.* This is the textbook "most common way to be right and make no money."

## S8 — Catalyst / time-to-convergence
The only real catalyst — a named volume foundry customer — is not pre-registrable on a forecastable date; it's binary and exogenous. The valuation gap (if any) has **no mechanism to close inside horizon** without that customer. A thesis with no convergence mechanism is a value trap. Gate result: **hold off.**

## S11 — People overlay (no-moat regime)
Intel currently has **no durable process moat** (TSMC holds it). Per *bet-on-people-where-no-moat-exists*, in a no-moat regime the thesis becomes **management focus**. The current posture (sharper, IFS-prioritized) is a positive but **unproven** — and any renewed attention-diversion (further restructurings, board churn, capex thrash) is a **sell signal**, not a buy.

## Call: **HOLD**
Not a Buy (edge predicate fails on forecastability and pricing; no in-horizon catalyst). Not a Sell (downside is real but the option value is non-zero and partly priced; shorting fights an embedded turnaround narrative with policy support). **Existing holders: hold the option; do not add. New money: no trade.** Pre-registered flip-to-Buy trigger: a **named, leading-edge external logic customer at volume on 18A/14A** — that single fact converts the thesis from unforecastable optionality into researchable edge.

*Most load-bearing: S3 conjunctive filter, S7 four-part edge predicate, S8 convergence requirement.*
