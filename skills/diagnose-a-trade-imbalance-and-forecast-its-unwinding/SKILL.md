# Diagnose a Trade Imbalance and Forecast Its Unwinding

## Spine
Trade and capital imbalances are not made by national virtue or villainy but forced by accounting identities: a surplus is a savings-investment gap exported, and because every surplus requires a matching deficit, the system is two-sided and conserved — so you reason from the identities, locate the policy that suppresses consumption to force savings, trace its forced mirror onto the counterparty, and conclude that the imbalance must reverse, leaving only the question of whether it reverses through orderly demand growth or through crisis, default, and trade war.

## Procedure

1. **Clear the moral/surface narrative off the table.** Before analyzing, refuse explanations that credit thrift/profligacy, "savings culture," deregulation, or any account that would equally explain the opposite outcome or every past episode. Discard a cause that fits both an outcome and its negation (skeptic-002), fade morally-framed causal stories (trader-004 / teacher-005), and falsify any single cause by finding the same effect where the cause is absent across eras (skeptic-001 / historian-002 / teacher-001). **Gate:** if the leading explanation survives a flip-test and a cross-era counter-case, it may be real causation — otherwise demote it to "surface narrative" and proceed to the identities. Build a cross-era reference class for "imbalance + crisis" to set your prior that this is textbook, not mysterious (historian-001).

2. **Anchor on the inviolable accounting identities.** Restate the situation in terms that are true by definition: trade balance = national savings − investment; every surplus is matched one-for-one by a deficit elsewhere; the current account and capital account sum to zero. Reason only by propagating these identities, not by narrative (quant-002 / teacher-003), and treat any conclusion they force as valid even when you cannot see the micro-path (quant-010 / teacher-010 / skeptic-022). **Gate:** if a claim contradicts an identity, the claim is wrong, not the identity — stop and re-derive (trader-001).

3. **Reclassify the surface "trade policy" as a savings-rate transfer.** Whatever the instrument — tariff, currency undervaluation, wage suppression, financial repression, consumption tax — recognize it works by shifting income from households to producers, which forces up the national savings rate and only thereby moves the trade balance; it does NOT work through making imports dearer (quant-005 / quant-004 — collapse logically-equivalent instruments to the one lever / teacher-009). Identify the single binding lever (the consumption-to-GDP wedge) behind the menu of policies, and separate which policies change the savings *level* vs. merely its composition (quant-034 / trader-016). **Gate:** if a proposed remedy does not move the savings-investment gap (e.g., "Greeks can't produce what they import, so devaluation won't help"), it cannot move the balance — reject it (skeptic-006 / quant-035).

4. **Confirm the diagnosis is mechanism, not tautology, with the differential-signature tests.** "A deficit country saves too little" is by definition true and therefore empty; demand a test that distinguishes rival causes by their differing cross-section / time-series / held-constant predictions (skeptic-005 / skeptic-007 / teacher-013 / quant-006). Stress-test the claimed mechanism by disabling its channel and checking the outcome still follows (skeptic-008), and decompose the aggregate to confirm a single-cause story survives disaggregation (skeptic-010 / quant-014). **Gate:** if the explanation is only an identity restated (a tautology), it has no forecasting content — keep it as bookkeeping but do not treat it as a cause (teacher-022 — residual vs. driver).

5. **Trace the forced mirror flow onto the counterparty (the two-sided step).** Model the world as one closed, conserved system: any country's forced rise in savings must export capital and import demand, forcing an equal-and-opposite savings-investment move abroad (quant-003 / teacher-004 / teacher-012). The counterparty (deficit country) cannot independently choose its savings rate once capital flows in — its savings, debt, bubble, or unemployment is an *induced residual*, not a choice (quant-040 / skeptic-024 / teacher-032). Treat the local balance as endogenous to the dominant counterparty's policy (trader-015). **Gate:** if you have "explained" one side without booking the obligatory opposite move on the other side, your analysis is incomplete — return to step 2.

6. **Classify the imbalance by the QUALITY of what funds it, and gate on sustainability.** Distinguish a capital inflow that funds productive investment (self-liquidating) from one that funds consumption, asset bubbles, or accumulating debt (distortion-sustained) (trader-003 / skeptic-032 / quant-046 — inflow value gated on return > servicing cost). Run the inflow through the "good-inflow conditions": it must raise productive investment, the return must exceed the servicing cost, and it must not merely inflate assets or be involuntarily absorbed (trader-024). Audit the celebrated success metric (reserves, growth, surplus) for hidden liabilities, deferred costs, and externalized risk — a hoard of reserves may be a fragility marker, not an achievement (skeptic-015 / skeptic-017 / trader-011 / skeptic-019). **Gate:** if the imbalance is distortion-sustained (debt/bubble-funded, return < cost), it is unsustainable → it WILL reverse; proceed to forecast. If it is genuinely self-liquidating, it may persist — say so and stop here.

7. **Locate the disabled regulator and the regime that removed it.** A chronic imbalance persists because the valve that would normally clear it (a floating currency, free wages, an interest-rate adjustment, a credible default mechanism) has been disabled by policy or institutional structure; name the missing regulator and the regime break that removed it (historian-011 / teacher-043). **Gate:** if you cannot identify a disabled valve, suspect the imbalance is not actually chronic/forced — recheck step 3.

8. **Separate the inevitable endpoint from the choosable path.** From the identities, the imbalance must unwind (inevitable); what is open is the *path* (quant-048 / teacher-052 / historian-009). Derive the constrained option set the identities permit — e.g., a surplus falls because exports drop OR imports rise; a deficit contracts under high unemployment OR low unemployment; savings rises via falling income OR rising income (quant-037 / teacher-036 / historian-009 — derive the exhaustive menu, then eliminate). Eliminate the politically/economically foreclosed options to a forced residual (trader-017). **Gate:** every survivor on the menu must still satisfy the identities; discard any that don't.

9. **Forecast the unwinding dynamics — orderly vs. crisis — and price the conflict.** Determine whether the adjustment runs through demand growth (orderly) or through unemployment, default, and capital-flight (crisis), recognizing the reversal can be a discontinuous snap once a confidence/belief threshold breaks rather than a smooth glide (trader-002 / quant-036 / teacher-037b — reflexive belief-triggered feedback). Map the crisis as a multi-agent self-reinforcing cascade of individually-rational but collectively-ruinous moves (teacher-037 / trader-019 / historian-007). Reframe one-sided blame as a two-sided adjustment problem and locate where the real leverage sits: in a demand-short world, the deficit country (holder of scarce demand) holds the cards, even though perception says otherwise — diagnose conflict risk from this real-vs-perceived leverage gap (teacher-038 / teacher-053 / trader-028). **Gate / branch:** if surplus countries absorb the adjustment via domestic demand → orderly rebalancing; if they resist → the burden snaps onto deficit countries as crisis + rising trade protection (a fallback branch, not an alternative the system can avoid).

10. **Pressure-test the standard counter-fears and "fixes" before concluding.** Apply the symmetric-law and relocation checks: a "fix" that only moves the imbalance to another partner under conservation is no fix (trader-023 / quant-038 / teacher-042); a feared creditor "funding pull" that is mechanically non-discretionary (the surplus country *must* recycle to hold its peg) is not a real threat (trader-021 / trader-005 — read the peg-maker's balance sheet as a flow signal); an "exorbitant privilege" can be re-derived from forced consequences as an exorbitant *burden* (teacher-040 / teacher-044 / quant-031). A postponement is deferral-with-interest, not immunity (trader-030). **Gate:** if your forecast relies on a popular fear that fails one of these checks, drop it and re-forecast.

## When this whole procedure misleads (guardrails)
- **The conserved-system / closed-world assumption can be wrong at the margin.** The identities bind globally and ex-post, but the spine treats one bilateral pair (e.g., US–China) as if it were the closed system; a multilateral world lets imbalances reroute rather than resolve, and lets a third party absorb shocks the two-sided model says are forced. Always re-check whether the relevant conserved aggregate is really closed at the boundary you drew.
- **Identity-true ≠ behaviorally-timed.** Identities say the imbalance *must* reverse and the mirror *must* exist; they say nothing about *when*, and "must eventually" can outlast a forecaster (and a position) by a decade. The spine systematically under-weights how long a disabled regulator can be held shut by political will.
- **It dismisses agency too fast.** By design the spine demotes "choices" to identity-forced residuals; this is its power and its blind spot — it can wave away genuine policy or productivity differences (real comparative advantage, real cultural savings preferences at normal levels) as mere residuals, mis-attributing a structural cause where a behavioral one is also operating.
- **Tables and micro-magnitudes are out of scope here.** The procedure tells you the *sign and direction* forced by the identities; it does not size the move. Sizing requires data this spine does not supply — do not fabricate magnitudes.
- **Reflexive-threshold forecasting is unfalsifiable in advance.** "It will snap when confidence breaks" cannot be timed and can rationalize any delay; treat the crisis branch as a contingency, not a dated prediction.

## Known gaps (procedure steps that need a method the library lacks)
- **Step 6 / Step 9 — magnitude & timing sizing.** The library supplies sign and direction (which way savings, balance, and flows must move) but no atom that *sizes* the gap or dates the reversal from data; `GAP: quantify-the-adjustment-magnitude-and-timing-from-flow-data`.
- **Step 1 — drawing the closed-system boundary.** No atom decides *which* set of countries/agents to treat as the conserved closed system; the spine assumes it. `GAP: choose-the-conserved-system-boundary-multilateral-vs-bilateral`.
- **Step 9 — political-feasibility weighting of the option menu.** Eliminating options to a forced residual (step 8→9) needs a way to weight political will and institutional capacity to hold a regulator shut; the library reasons economically, not politically. `GAP: weight-option-menu-by-political-feasibility-and-time-to-act`.
