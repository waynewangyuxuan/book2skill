# Follow the transfer — decode a policy by who it taxes and subsidizes

## When to use
When a policy is debated by its stated purpose, but its real effect is a wealth transfer between groups.

## Method
1. Ignore the policy's label and stated intent.
2. Identify the price/rate it moves and the parties on either side of that move.
3. Read it as a tax on whoever pays the worse price and a subsidy to whoever gets the better one.
4. Collapse multiple differently-named measures into one family if they all move the same intermediate variable; then scan the domain for hidden members that also move it.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: a policy; the price/rate it moves; the affected parties. Outputs: a tax-side / subsidy-side map and the family of equivalent measures.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Second-order incidence can differ from first-order; trace who ultimately bears it.
- The "single variable" framing must genuinely capture all members.

## Worked example
A measure marketed as financial stability is decoded as a transfer from depositors to borrowers via a suppressed deposit rate.
