## When to use
Soliciting feedback on your work where an open-ended ask would yield only polite reassurance.

## Method
1. Do not ask "how did I do?" — it yields vague praise.
2. Specify rated dimensions and ask the giver to score each.
3. Anchor to an exemplar with "how could I be more like [exemplar]?" to surface the gap concretely.
4. Convert the scores and the gap-to-exemplar into specific, actionable changes.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: your work; rated dimensions; an exemplar.
- Outputs: scored, exemplar-anchored, actionable critique.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Asking open-ended and getting useless reassurance.
- Choosing an exemplar the giver does not know.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
Instead of "was the note good?", a practitioner asks for 1–5 scores on clarity, novelty, and rigor plus how to make it more like a benchmark note, and gets concrete fixes.
