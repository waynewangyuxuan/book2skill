## When to use
Reading whether a person is being candid in an interview or negotiation.

## Method
1. Give more weight to signals the person controls least and can least fake than to the polished, managed channels.
2. Treat every cue as probabilistic, never deterministic — a single tell is a hypothesis to probe, not a conclusion.
3. Probe the hypothesis with follow-up rather than acting on the cue.
4. Stay neutral and poker-faced so you do not reveal what you noticed and shut the source down.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: a live interaction; observable managed and unmanaged channels.
- Outputs: a probabilistic honesty read plus probes to resolve it.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Over-reading one cue into certainty.
- Reacting visibly and closing the channel.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A confident scripted answer is discounted in favor of an off-script hesitation, which the practitioner then probes without showing reaction.
