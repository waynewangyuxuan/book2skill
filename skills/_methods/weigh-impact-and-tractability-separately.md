## When to use
Prioritizing which drivers to research when some are easy to quantify and others are large but hard to forecast.

## Method
1. Score each driver on impact (how much it moves the outcome) and tractability (how forecastable its trigger is) as two separate axes.
2. Do not let tractability cap importance — a large but hard-to-forecast force still dominates the outcome.
3. Prefer drivers whose triggers diligent research can actually forecast among comparable-impact candidates, but never drop a dominant driver because it is hard.
4. For the high-impact / low-tractability drivers, carry their uncertainty explicitly rather than ignoring them.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: candidate drivers scored on impact and tractability.
- Outputs: a prioritization that keeps dominant-but-hard drivers in view.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Researching only the easily-quantified drivers and missing the one that decides the outcome.
- Treating an unforecastable dominant force as if it were unimportant.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A practitioner keeps a hard-to-forecast macro driver central to the analysis instead of over-investing in a precisely-measurable but minor one.
