# Adjudicate causal direction by differential signature

## When to use
When two hypotheses explain the same correlation but imply opposite causal directions, and the correlation alone cannot separate them.

## Method
1. State each rival hypothesis explicitly.
2. Derive the distinct fingerprint each would leave in some other observable (a marker the two predict differently).
3. Read that marker.
4. Keep the hypothesis whose signature matches; discard the other.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: rival causal directions; a separating marker. Outputs: the surviving direction.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- The marker must be genuinely predicted differently by the two hypotheses.
- A third unconsidered cause can mimic either signature.

## Worked example
Two stories about why a price and a quantity co-move are separated by checking whether reserves were accumulating or running down — a marker only one story predicts.
