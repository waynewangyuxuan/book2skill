## When to use
Before acting on a correct-but-static view, to ensure the mispricing will actually resolve in your horizon.

## Method
1. Require an identified catalyst before acting; a correct view with no trigger is not actionable.
2. Grade the catalyst on four axes: material, under-appreciated, forecastable by effort, and in your horizon.
3. Pre-register the catalyst and the move it predicts as a falsifiable thesis test.
4. If the catalyst fires but the expected move does not occur, treat that as a falsification signal and re-underwrite the thesis.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: a thesis; a candidate catalyst; its grade on the four axes.
- Outputs: a pre-registered catalyst-and-predicted-move that disciplines later judgment.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Acting on conviction with no catalyst, then waiting indefinitely.
- Ignoring a fired-but-no-move signal and rationalizing the hold.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A pre-registered catalyst fires but the price does not move; the practitioner re-underwrites and finds the thesis was already priced.
