## When to use
When a player's strengths are being cited as the basis of a sustainable edge and you must separate structural moats from copyable competence.

## Method
1. List each claimed strength (systems, talent, practices, processes, brand).
2. For each, ask: can a determined, well-funded rival replicate it — hire the talent, buy the system, emulate the practice — given enough spending?
3. If yes, classify it as operational effectiveness, not a barrier. It can make you better but cannot block rivals.
4. Only strengths rooted in the market's structure, that rivals cannot replicate no matter their spending, count as genuine advantages.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Input: a list of a player's strengths.
- Output: each strength labeled replicable (operational) or structural (a real barrier).

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Failure: counting an admired but copyable competence as a moat.
- Assumption: "replicable in principle by a well-funded rival" is the right bar; secrecy/lead-time only delays, it does not make a strength structural.
