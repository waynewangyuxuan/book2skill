## When to use
Deciding which of many candidate drivers are worth tracking and modeling.

## Method
1. Apply a conjunctive filter to each candidate driver: in horizon AND material (past a pre-set threshold) AND has a forecastable, not-yet-priced catalyst.
2. Drop any driver that fails any clause.
3. Among the survivors, score each on magnitude-of-impact and forecastability-of-trigger.
4. Ruthlessly cull to the top two-to-four; track those and let the rest go.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: candidate drivers; horizon; materiality threshold; catalyst forecastability.
- Outputs: a culled set of two-to-four tracked drivers.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Tracking a material driver that has no forecastable catalyst.
- Keeping too many drivers and diluting focus.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A dozen candidate drivers pass to three after the conjunctive filter, and the practitioner tracks only those three.
