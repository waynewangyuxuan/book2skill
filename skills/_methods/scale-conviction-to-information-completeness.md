## When to use
Setting conviction (and therefore sizing) on a decision where your information is necessarily incomplete.

## Method
1. Estimate what fraction of the decision-relevant information you actually hold.
2. Treat your set as a known-incomplete fraction of the whole, not as the whole.
3. Size conviction to the estimated completeness, not to the apparent strength of what you happen to have.
4. Where completeness is low, widen the range and reduce size accordingly.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: the available information; an estimate of its completeness.
- Outputs: a conviction level scaled to completeness.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Feeling certain from a rich-looking but partial information set.
- Mis-estimating how much of the relevant whole you hold.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A practitioner holds detailed data on one segment but recognizes it is a small slice of the decision and sizes conviction down accordingly.
