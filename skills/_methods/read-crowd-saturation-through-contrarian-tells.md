## When to use
Timing entries and exits where crowd sentiment may be exhausted, as an overlay on a fundamental view.

## Method
1. Watch for saturation tells: near-unanimity, "this time is different" narratives, too-far-too-fast moves, and non-reaction to confirming news.
2. Read clustered bullish saturation as a sell signal and capitulation as a buy signal.
3. Map the ownership base by investor style to anticipate where air-pockets (forced sellers) and floors (sticky holders) sit.
4. Layer these signals on top of fundamentals; never let them replace the fundamental view.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: sentiment indicators; price action; ownership-by-style data.
- Outputs: a contrarian timing overlay with anticipated air-pockets/floors.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Fading a trend on sentiment alone with no fundamental support.
- Calling saturation too early in a durable move.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
Unanimous bullishness and no reaction to good news signal exhaustion; the practitioner trims into the saturation rather than chasing.
