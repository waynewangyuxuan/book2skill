## When to use
Evaluating a well-funded, well-regarded actor's move into an adjacent market where it lacks a structural advantage, or forecasting such an entry's outcome.

## Method
1. Check the entrant's structural position in the TARGET arena (not its overall stature): does it hold a barrier there?
2. If not, classify the move as "deep-pocketed incumbent buys/finances its way into an arena where it has no barrier" and assign a strong negative prior regardless of brand, size, or balance sheet — capital is not itself a moat.
3. Watch for the boomerang sub-mechanism: aggressive financing/inventory deployed to force entry often turns against the entrant (e.g. own product re-entering via gray market, customer credit becoming bad debt).
4. Forecast by base rate, not by the entrant's prestige.

## Inputs → Outputs
- In: a capital-rich actor + a target arena + its barrier status there.
- Out: a base-rate-informed (likely negative) prognosis and identified boomerang risks.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- If the entrant can transport or build a genuine barrier in the target arena, the negative prior weakens.
- The incumbent being attacked must itself be reasonably run; the base rate is about deep pockets alone failing, not about invincible defenders.
