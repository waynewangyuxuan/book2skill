## When to use
Forecasting whether a rival will fight or cooperate, or projecting an organization's behavior beyond its current leadership.

## Method
1. Find the metric the actor's people are actually rewarded on (not its rhetoric).
2. Predict behavior from the metric: rewards on market share / growth-relative-to-rivals → relentless defection (relative performance is zero-sum); rewards on absolute profitability / return on capital → cooperation feasible.
3. To shift a rivalry toward cooperation, change the reward metric — that is the lever.
4. Treat organizational culture as a slow-moving, mean-reverting state variable: an out-of-character regime (e.g. cooperation imposed by particular leaders) is likely to revert once they depart. Weight base-culture over current-leadership when projecting beyond the current tenure; durable change requires altering selection/incentives, not swapping a figurehead.

## Inputs → Outputs
- In: an actor + its internal reward metric + leadership tenure.
- Out: a forecast of its cooperativeness and a prediction of reversion after leadership change.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Stated metrics may differ from truly operative incentives; read what is rewarded in practice.
- Strong external shocks can override culture temporarily.
- Mean reversion has uncertain timing; "it will revert" is more reliable than "when."
