## When to use
Before committing to a high-stakes decision, and periodically while holding it.

## Method
1. Answer a fixed pre-decision questionnaire: What is my non-consensus insight? Why is it missed by others? What is the catalyst? What could go wrong (pre-mortem)?
2. Document the thesis and its parameters contemporaneously so the record cannot be silently rewritten.
3. Periodically subject the held thesis to a "would I make this exact call today?" test against the documented original parameters.
4. Treat a failed re-decision as a signal to exit, not to rationalize.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: the candidate decision; the fixed questionnaire; a contemporaneous record.
- Outputs: a documented decision and periodic from-scratch re-decisions.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Skipping the pre-mortem and being blindsided by a known risk.
- Letting hindsight quietly rewrite the original thesis.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A pre-mortem surfaces a failure mode the analyst had ignored, changing the sizing before any commitment.
