## When to use
When two terms are treated as separate variables but seem suspiciously linked, or when an analysis double-counts.

## Method
1. State each concept as a proposition.
2. Check whether one is logically equivalent to the other viewed from a different vantage point (insider vs outsider, supply vs demand, cause vs effect).
3. If "X can do what others cannot" is the same fact as "others are blocked from doing X," collapse the two terms.
4. Reason about the single underlying object; discard the redundant variable.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Input: two concepts presented as distinct.
- Output: either a confirmed identity (collapse to one) or a confirmed real distinction (keep both, knowing why).

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Failure: collapsing concepts that merely correlate but are not logically equivalent.
- Assumption: equivalence must hold by definition/logic, not just empirically.
