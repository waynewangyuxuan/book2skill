# Conclude from an identity even when the path is invisible

## When to use
When an accounting/conservation identity pins an endpoint, but an objector resists because the intermediate mechanism is untraceable or counterintuitive.

## Method
1. State the binding identity and the endpoint it forces.
2. Separate the certainty of the endpoint (guaranteed by the identity) from the route (which may be opaque).
3. Treat "I can't see how the adjustment happens" as a gap in the observer's model, not as evidence against the conclusion.
4. If asked, demonstrate at least one feasible route, but do not make the conclusion contingent on enumerating all routes.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: a binding identity; a forced endpoint; an objection-from-untraceability. Outputs: the endpoint asserted with identity-level certainty, route flagged as open.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- The "identity" must be genuinely exact, not approximate.
- Do not use this to wave away a real behavioral assumption disguised as an identity.

## Worked example
A net resource flow must reverse for a debt to be repaid; the conclusion holds even though no observer can name in advance the specific transactions that will accomplish the reversal.
