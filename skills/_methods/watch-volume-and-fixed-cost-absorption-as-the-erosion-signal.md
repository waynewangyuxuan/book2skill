## When to use
Monitoring a position in a differentiated business for the first signs that its pricing power / differentiation is failing.

## Method
1. Do not wait for visible price cuts as your sell signal. The early signature of erosion from fragmenting entry is: stable prices + falling unit share + rising fixed-cost-per-unit. Track volume and fixed-cost-absorption metrics, not just price.
2. Treat persistent frontline / customer-facing reports that "customers no longer pay our premium" as the authoritative signal the differentiation moat has closed — even when leadership insists the next product cycle will restore it.
3. When the edge is structurally gone, switch the response from "engineer/differentiate our way out" to attacking the cost structure. Weight ground-truth demand signals above internal narrative.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: unit-volume trend; fixed-cost-per-unit trend; sticker-price trend; recurring sales-force / channel reports on willingness-to-pay.
- Outputs: an early erosion alert and a directive to pivot from differentiation to cost discipline.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- A price-watcher will miss this entirely — the danger is invisible in price and shows only in volume/absorption.
- Founder/engineer-led cultures tend to discount frontline price-resistance and bet on the next product; that delay is the failure mode.
- Assumes you can get honest volume and fixed-cost data and unfiltered frontline reports.
