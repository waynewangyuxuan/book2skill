## When to use
You need to define the boundaries of a "market" before analyzing competition, and taxonomic/intuitive boundaries are arbitrary or contested.

## Method
1. For each candidate segment, list the leading players (by share).
2. Compare rosters across adjacent segments.
3. Merge segments whose dominant-player lists substantially overlap; keep separate those whose lists are disjoint — distinct dominant-player sets imply distinct markets requiring separate analysis.
4. Use this shared-competitor-identity test in place of arbitrary category boundaries; let boundaries be discovered from the data.

## Inputs → Outputs
- In: candidate segments + their leading players.
- Out: an empirically-grounded market map (which segments are one market vs several).

## Failure modes / assumptions
- A single firm appearing in two segments for incidental reasons can mislead; weight the whole roster, not one name.
- Rosters shift over time; the map is a snapshot.
- Vertical (supply-chain) adjacency differs from horizontal substitution — this overlap test is for the latter.
