## When to use
When you need to forecast the long-run equilibrium state of an open, low-friction system (a market, a queue, an arbitrage) and want a derivation you can re-run rather than a slogan.

## Method
1. Start from the disequilibrium signal (e.g. above-normal returns / unusual opportunity).
2. Ask who notices and what they do: actors flow in or expand.
3. Trace the mechanical consequence: the resource fragments across more actors; per-unit advantage shrinks (costs rise and/or price falls).
4. Iterate until the disequilibrium signal disappears.
5. The stopping point is the equilibrium — for an open market, expected economic profit at zero regardless of differentiation or skill.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Input: a system with free entry and an above-normal return signal.
- Output: the equilibrium state, derived step by step.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Failure: stopping the chain early or ignoring exit/contraction asymmetries.
- Assumption: entry is genuinely unrestricted; if a barrier exists, the chain halts before zero profit (use the structural-advantage audit instead).
