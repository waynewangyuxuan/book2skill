## When to use
Framing analysis for an audience whose evaluation method and values may differ from your own.

## Method
1. Directly research what the audience values instead of assuming; producers often value different things than audiences do.
2. Frame the conclusion using the method the audience actually uses to judge, holding any theoretically-superior method as a private cross-check.
3. Master the audience's professional vocabulary; fluent use of a field's language signals membership and credibility.
4. Being right by an unshared method still loses — translate into their frame.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: the audience's values, evaluation method, and vocabulary.
- Outputs: a conclusion framed in the audience's method and language.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Presenting in your preferred framework that the audience does not act on.
- Guessing audience preferences instead of researching them.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A practitioner reframes a DCF-based call into the multiple-based frame the audience actually uses to decide, and it lands.
