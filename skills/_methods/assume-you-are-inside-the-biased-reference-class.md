## When to use
Designing any process meant to resist your own cognitive biases.

## Method
1. Accept that cognitive errors are universal and intelligence-independent; assume you are inside the biased reference class.
2. Treat the belief "it never happens to me" as the gateway bias to defeat first.
3. Instrument against bias with mechanical tools — contemporaneous logs, forced disconfirmation steps, a scaled proof burden — not felt objectivity or willpower.
4. Build routines that correct for accessibility, asymmetric attribution, and prior-experience aversion.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: your decision process; known bias categories.
- Outputs: mechanical debiasing instruments built into the process.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Relying on willpower or self-assessed objectivity.
- Believing intelligence exempts you from bias.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
Rather than trusting their objectivity, a practitioner keeps a decision log and a forced disconfirmation checklist that catches a bias they would have denied having.
