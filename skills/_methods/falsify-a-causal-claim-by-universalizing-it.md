# Falsify a causal claim by universalizing it

## When to use
When someone asserts a local causal mechanism ("more of X here gives more Y") that, if true, would imply a general law.

## Method
1. Promote the local claim into the universal monotonic relationship it logically implies.
2. Identify the cross-sectional pattern that law requires across all cases.
3. Compare against the data.
4. If the pattern is absent or reversed, reject the original claim.
5. Variant: grant the opponent's premise and re-derive; if it strengthens the action it was meant to attack, the argument is self-defeating.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: a local causal claim; cross-sectional data. Outputs: a keep/reject verdict.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- The implied universal law must follow genuinely from the claim, not from a strawman of it.
- Threshold/nonlinear effects can defeat a naive monotonicity test.

## Worked example
"Holding this reserve asset is a burden" is universalized into "all holders should shed it"; observing that holders consistently acquire it instead refutes the claim.
