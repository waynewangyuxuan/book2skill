## When to use
A business is outperforming and you must decide whether and where to expand it.

## Method
1. Attribute the margin gap to one of three buckets before acting:
   - Transferable management/culture → you may replicate anywhere.
   - Non-replicable local input costs → stay put; fill in the current territory.
   - Structural advantage (e.g. local economies of scale) → expand only into geographies/product spaces where that same structure can be re-established.
   The same outperformance implies opposite actions depending on the source.
2. Sequence any expansion as contiguous outward steps from your area of dominance. Consolidate share in each new ring before advancing.
3. Refuse discontinuous leaps into distant markets owned by entrenched incumbents — those force you to fight at a structural disadvantage on someone else's turf. A distant great market can be the wrong move purely because it is non-contiguous.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: a decomposition of your cost/margin advantage by source; a map of contiguous vs distant expansion targets; incumbent strength in each target.
- Outputs: an expand-anywhere / stay-put / expand-where-structure-repeats decision, plus a contiguous roll-out sequence.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Skipping the attribution step is the core error: people read "we're winning" as "expand everywhere," which fails when the edge is local or structural.
- Leapfrogging into a distant stronghold dilutes the advantage that produced the outperformance.
- Misattributing local-cost or structural edges to "great management" causes over-expansion.
