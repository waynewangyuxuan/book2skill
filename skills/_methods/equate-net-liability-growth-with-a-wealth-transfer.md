# Equate net-liability growth with a wealth transfer

## When to use
When comparing an explicit asset transfer against debt accumulation as ways to shift wealth between sectors, or when a transfer is denied because no asset visibly changed hands.

## Method
1. Track a sector's net position (liabilities minus assets) over time.
2. Recognize a growing net-liability as a wealth transfer out of that sector, of the same sign as an explicit asset transfer.
3. Measure the transfer as the change in the net-liability position.
4. Treat debt accumulation and asset transfer as substitute levers with the same quantified immediate effect.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: a sector's assets and liabilities over time. Outputs: the implied wealth transfer = change in net-liability.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- The two levers differ in side effects (debt becomes a future constraint) even if the immediate transfer matches.
- Asset revaluation affects the net figure.

## Worked example
A sector whose debt rises faster than its assets is shown to have suffered a wealth transfer equal to the net-liability increase, with no explicit asset sale.
