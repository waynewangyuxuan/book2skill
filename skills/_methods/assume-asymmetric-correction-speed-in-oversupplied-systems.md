## When to use
Forecasting how quickly a market with excess capacity / oversupply will rebalance, especially with durable assets and reluctant operators.

## Method
1. Note that entry/expansion and exit/contraction are structurally asymmetric: adding capacity is fast and eager; removing it is slow and resisted.
2. Lengthen the expected overhang in proportion to asset longevity and capital intensity — durable assets and emotionally/financially committed operators extend the glut.
3. Assign a low base rate to "this distressed, oversupplied market recovers quickly," and discount narratives that assume rapid self-correction.

## Inputs → Outputs
- In: a market in oversupply + its asset durability and operator commitment.
- Out: a longer, asymmetry-adjusted recovery timeline.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Forced exits (bankruptcy waves, regulation) can accelerate contraction beyond the base rate.
- Short-asset-life / low-capital industries correct faster — the asymmetry is weaker there.
