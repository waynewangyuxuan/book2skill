## When to use
Before reinvesting, expanding, or valuing growth in any business or project.

## Method
1. Determine the return the actor earns on invested resources relative to its cost of capital.
2. Classify:
   - **Destroys value** — returns below cost of capital. The more energetically you grow, the more value you destroy.
   - **Neutral** — returns equal cost of capital (no advantage). All gains accrue to the new capital; existing owners get nothing. Leaving growth out of the valuation is correct.
   - **Creates value** — returns above cost of capital, which requires a sustainable competitive advantage in a growing space.
3. So "should we grow?" reduces to "do we have a durable advantage here?" Pursue growth only inside the moat.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Input: a growth/reinvestment opportunity and the actor's return-vs-cost-of-capital position.
- Output: a verdict (grow / indifferent / don't grow) and whether growth belongs in the valuation.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Failure: chasing growth for its own sake while earning below the cost of capital.
- Assumption: the cost of capital and achievable return are estimable; a claimed durable advantage has been independently confirmed.
