## When to use
Analyzing a multi-party system (negotiation, value chain, oligopoly, alliance) to find the maximum achievable joint value, the cooperation levers, and your own bargaining power — even when full coordination is unattainable.

## Method
1. Run the thought experiment: how would the entire system behave if one rational intelligence ran it to maximize total value?
2. Enumerate every coordination dimension it would optimize as a checklist of joint-improvement opportunities: capacity level and siting, allocating production to lowest-cost nodes, procurement, distribution-overlap, R&D de-duplication, product/niche coverage, advertising clutter, shared overhead, risk pooling.
3. Locate each real player in that ideal: what role and fair share would it have?
4. Read off leverage: a player essential to the ideal has bargaining power; a player with no role in it has none and survives only on others' failure to coordinate.
5. Use the gap between ideal and actual to size the achievable surplus and identify the cooperative moves worth pursuing — partial moves toward it still capture value.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: a system of interdependent players and the activities they perform + its current configuration.
- Outputs: the maximum joint value, the list of coordination levers, the size of the achievable surplus, and each player's leverage / survival outlook.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- It is a diagnostic and design target, not an immediately achievable plan or a forecast; full coordination is usually unattainable.
- Feasibility constraints bound the achievable set.
- Coordinating some dimensions (notably pricing) can cross antitrust lines; many other dimensions are safe.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
For a fragmented value chain, imagining one owner reveals it would route output to the lowest-cost plant, de-duplicate R&D, and assign non-overlapping niches — the gap from reality sizes the surplus, and any link with no role in that optimum is shown to survive only on rivals' failure to coordinate.
