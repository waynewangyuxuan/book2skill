## When to use
Judging a source's competence and honesty from how specific their answers are.

## Method
1. Read vagueness as a flag for a bluffer or someone outside their competence.
2. Do not run the inference in reverse — fluent, precise numbers are easy to fabricate and do not confer honesty.
3. Use specificity only to detect ignorance, never to confer trust.
4. Verify the substance independently regardless of how detailed the answer was.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: a source's answers and their level of specificity.
- Outputs: an ignorance flag (from vagueness) plus a reminder to verify the specific claims.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Trusting a confident, detailed answer because detail feels like knowledge.
- Dismissing a vague but honest "I don't know" as useless when it is a calibration signal.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A source rattles off precise figures that turn out fabricated; the practitioner had withheld trust pending verification despite the fluency.
