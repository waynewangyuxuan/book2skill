## When to use
When diagnosing a retention problem or designing for stickiness, and you need to know which lever actually holds people.

## Method
1. Decompose stickiness into three first-principle reasons a party won't leave:
   - **Habit:** frequent, near-automatic repurchase that bypasses reflection.
   - **Switching costs:** time/money/effort/risk required to change provider.
   - **Search costs:** difficulty and risk of finding and vetting a replacement.
2. Diagnose which one(s) actually apply (often only one).
3. Match the reinforcement move to the active mechanism: increase purchase frequency to build habit; deepen integration to raise switching costs; add complexity/customization to raise search costs.
4. Note that habit attaches to a single product, not a whole portfolio — do not assume it transfers across offerings.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Input: a customer/user relationship.
- Output: the active stickiness mechanism(s) and the matched reinforcement tactic.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Failure: treating brand recognition or differentiation as stickiness — those alone do not hold anyone.
- Assumption: the three buckets cover the retention causes for your domain; verify before designing.
