# Test whether self-differentiation escapes a shared-mechanism risk

## When to use
When a party claims it will avoid a system-wide outcome because it is the responsible/virtuous exception.

## Method
1. Identify the mechanism driving the system-wide risk.
2. Ask whether it is shared-structural (hits all members) or member-specific.
3. Demand that the claimed exception point to a difference in the mechanism, not in reputation or intent.
4. If the mechanism is shared, reject the decoupling claim — signaling virtue does not provide an exit.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: the decoupling claim; the risk mechanism. Outputs: escape-possible vs no-escape verdict.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- A genuine mechanistic difference does grant escape — don't over-apply.
- The shared mechanism must be correctly identified.

## Worked example
A member of a currency bloc claiming it will be spared a system-wide crunch is rejected because the crunch runs through the shared monetary mechanism it cannot exit.
