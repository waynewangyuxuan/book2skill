## When to use
When the single figure you need is forbidden, unknown, or undisclosed, but its components are individually obtainable.

## Method
1. Decompose the withheld answer into a set of innocuous sub-facts that sources will give.
2. Collect the fragments from multiple independent angles.
3. Compute the target conclusion yourself by simple arithmetic on the fragments.
4. Cross-validate by reconstructing the same conclusion via a second independent fragment-path.
5. Optionally present the assembled conclusion to a knowledgeable party to refute; a failed refutation corroborates it.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: a decomposable target; obtainable sub-facts; independent paths.
- Outputs: a synthesized figure no single source disclosed, with a corroboration check.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- A single fragment-path with no independent cross-check can compound an error.
- Over-reading fragments into a conclusion they cannot support.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
Denied a unit's revenue directly, a practitioner multiplies separately-disclosed unit volume and price to derive it, then checks it against a cost-side estimate.
