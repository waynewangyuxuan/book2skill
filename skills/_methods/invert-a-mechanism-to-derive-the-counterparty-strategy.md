## When to use
After analyzing any equilibrium or mechanism, when you want the playbook for the party that loses under it.

## Method
1. State the mechanism and identify who benefits from the resulting equilibrium.
2. Ask explicitly: who is harmed by this equilibrium?
3. For each enabling condition of the mechanism, derive its inverse as a lever for the harmed party.
4. Assemble those inverses into the counter-strategy.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Input: an understood mechanism/equilibrium and its enabling conditions.
- Output: an actionable counter-strategy for the disadvantaged party.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Failure: analyzing only from the beneficiary's side and missing the symmetric counter-play.
- Assumption: the enabling conditions are individually invertible by the harmed party.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
If sellers sustain high prices via transparent uniform public pricing and staying coordinated, a buyer inverts it: demand private non-transparent deals, negotiate individually, and reward whichever seller defects.
