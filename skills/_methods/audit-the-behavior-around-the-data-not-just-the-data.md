## When to use
Assessing whether reported figures are being managed, when the numbers themselves look clean.

## Method
1. Watch the behavior around the data, not only the data: changes in disclosure, willingness to answer, and access.
2. Treat a retreat from transparency (obscuring behavior, reduced detail) as a soft warning correlated with concealment, even absent a provable violation.
3. Benchmark discretionary choices against the peer group; being the lone outlier in a practice is itself a signal.
4. Weight these transparency-delta signals alongside, and sometimes above, the reported figures.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: disclosure behavior over time; peer-group practices.
- Outputs: a concealment-likelihood read from behavior, not just numbers.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Trusting clean-looking numbers while ignoring a disclosure retreat.
- Over-reading a benign change in reporting format as concealment.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A party quietly stops breaking out a previously-disclosed line and grows evasive on calls; the transparency retreat flags concern before any figure does.
