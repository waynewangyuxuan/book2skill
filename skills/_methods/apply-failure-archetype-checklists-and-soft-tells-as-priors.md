## When to use
Screening candidates (theses, ventures, teams) where recurring failure patterns and soft red flags carry predictive weight.

## Method
1. Maintain a memorized library of recurring failure classes in the domain.
2. Pattern-match each candidate against the library.
3. Honor qualitative red-flag tells, weighting against a candidate that matches a failure class absent specific countervailing evidence.
4. Treat the operating team's ability to execute as a dominant risk factor; an attractive plan attached to an unproven or distrusted team warrants low conviction regardless of the plan's appeal.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: a candidate; the failure-archetype library; execution-capability evidence.
- Outputs: a prior weighted by matched failure patterns and execution risk.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Ignoring a clear failure-pattern match because the upside is alluring.
- Over-applying an archetype to a genuine exception (require countervailing evidence).

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A plan matches a known failure archetype and is attached to an unproven team; the practitioner assigns low conviction despite the appealing thesis.
