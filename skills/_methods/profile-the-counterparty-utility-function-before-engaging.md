## When to use
Before entering a defended market or any contest where the other party's reaction decides your outcome.

## Method
1. Profile the counterparty's likely reaction by asking three questions:
   - Is it focused (all eggs in this basket) or diversified (many fronts to defend)?
   - Is it driven by rational financial returns (will accommodate when fighting is unprofitable) or by mission/identity (will fight to the death regardless of cost)?
   - Does it have anywhere else to go?
2. A focused, mission-driven, cornered party will retaliate against any encroachment, even small — so measured, low-profile entry won't appease it.
3. Choose your move from its utility function, not just its size: against such a party the only rational option may be to stay out entirely; against a coolly rational, diversified party a restrained entry can work.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Input: a counterparty and observable evidence of its focus, motivation, and alternatives.
- Output: a predicted reaction and a go/no-go or entry-style decision keyed to it.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Failure: judging only by who is stronger and ignoring that a fanatical defender will fight even a losing battle.
- Assumption: motivation is inferable from history and structure; when unclear, weight toward the more aggressive reading.
