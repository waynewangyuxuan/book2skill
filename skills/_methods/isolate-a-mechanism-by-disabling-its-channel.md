# Isolate a mechanism by disabling its channel

## When to use
When an outcome is casually attributed to one obvious channel and you want to know whether that channel is actually doing the work.

## Method
1. Identify the channel everyone assumes is responsible.
2. Construct an extreme toy case in which that channel cannot operate at all.
3. Re-run the reasoning in the toy world.
4. Whatever effect survives the channel being switched off is the true mechanism; if the effect vanishes, the channel was load-bearing.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: an outcome; the assumed channel. Outputs: confirmation or refutation that the channel drives the outcome.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- The extreme assumption must not accidentally disable a second real channel.
- Toy-world conclusions need a sanity check back in the realistic regime.

## Worked example
Setting a transfer's volume to zero shows the headline outcome still appears, proving the cited channel was not the cause.
