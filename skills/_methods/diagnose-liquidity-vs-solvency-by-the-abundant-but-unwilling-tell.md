# Diagnose liquidity vs solvency by the abundant-but-unwilling tell

## When to use
When a party is said to "need capital" and an outside injection is proposed as the fix.

## Method
1. Check whether the resource is genuinely scarce nearby, or abundant but withheld.
2. If nearby capital is abundant yet unwilling to fund the borrower, infer the problem is solvency/credibility, not liquidity.
3. Conclude that fresh outside lenders cannot fix a solvency problem — they would just join the avoidance.
4. Match the remedy to the real diagnosis: restore credibility/restructure, not inject liquidity.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: the stated "need"; the local availability and willingness of capital. Outputs: liquidity vs solvency diagnosis and the matching remedy.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Local unwillingness can have non-solvency causes (regulation, segmentation).
- A true liquidity crunch can masquerade as solvency in a panic.

## Worked example
A borrower surrounded by idle local capital that refuses to lend is diagnosed insolvent, so an outside bailout would only transfer the loss, not cure it.
