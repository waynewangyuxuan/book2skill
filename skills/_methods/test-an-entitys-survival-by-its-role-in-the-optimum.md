## When to use
Stress-testing the long-run viability of a weak or disadvantaged actor (e.g. a high-cost, redundant supplier).

## Method
1. Construct the fully-optimized configuration of the actor's system (use the single-coordinator thought experiment).
2. Ask whether the optimum has a genuine role for this actor or would route around / eliminate it.
3. If it would be eliminated, conclude its continued existence is contingent on the inefficiency, irrationality, or non-cooperation of stronger players — a precarious, time-limited footing.
4. Derive its only viable strategies: urgently improve its structural position, and actively prevent the stronger players from learning to cooperate (since their coordination is what ends it).

## Inputs → Outputs
- In: an actor + the optimized configuration of its system.
- Out: a survival verdict (does it exist in the optimum?) and, if not, the time-limited survival strategy.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Stronger players may never coordinate (common), giving a longer reprieve than the test implies — but it remains a fragile basis.
- The optimized configuration is a model; real frictions may preserve a role the idealization removes.
