## When to use
When a decision feels ethically questionable and you need to test it against your own rationalizations.

## Method
1. Run the decision through a battery of perspective-shift tests, each neutralizing a different distortion:
   - Publicity test — would it survive being made public?
   - Peer-review test — would respected peers endorse it?
   - Long-horizon test — how will it look years from now?
   - Admired-exemplar test — would someone you admire do it?
   - Outsider-explainability test — can you comfortably explain it to an intelligent outsider who lacks the "we've always done it this way" bias?
2. Treat failure of any test as a strong signal the action is wrong.
3. Once you have decided the hard right action, execute fast and make an irreversible move before pressure erodes your resolve.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: a questionable decision; the battery of tests.
- Outputs: a tested decision, locked in irreversibly if it is the hard right choice.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Rationalizing past a single failed test.
- Deciding right but delaying until pressure reverses it.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A decision that fails the publicity and outsider-explainability tests is dropped; a hard but right one is executed immediately before resolve weakens.
