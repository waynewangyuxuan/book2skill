## When to use
When judging how a value (profit, share, margin, performance) is being eroded and the obvious metric looks stable.

## Method
1. Enumerate the distinct channels through which the outcome can degrade, not just the salient one.
2. For a profit-style outcome, model at least two: (a) the price/headline channel everyone watches; (b) the cost-spreading channel — fragmentation holds the headline number steady but spreads fixed costs over fewer units, raising unit cost.
3. Check each channel separately, especially in fixed-cost-heavy settings where the headline metric can look fine while damage accumulates in the cost line.
4. Conclude only after every channel is checked.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Input: an outcome believed stable or eroding, plus its cost/volume structure.
- Output: the full set of active erosion channels and which one is doing the hidden damage.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Failure: declaring health because the watched metric (price) held, while a second channel quietly drained the result.
- Assumption: you can decompose the outcome into independent mechanisms; in fixed-cost businesses the cost-spreading channel is the usual blind spot.
