## When to use
As the top-level scaffold whenever you formulate strategy for any entity from scratch, tying the analytic pieces together.

## Method
1. **Locate yourself.** Map the competitive universe and determine whether you hold a sustainable advantage / sit behind a barrier to entry (use the structural-advantage audit).
2. **If advantaged, manage the interactions.** Handle the few rivals whose reactions affect you, using the interaction tools — equilibrium tests, the game templates, de-escalation, the cooperative-ideal scaffold.
3. **Always set a clear, simple, accurate vision** of where to go, so management attention can focus on getting there efficiently — this applies whether or not you have an advantage.
4. Treat strategy (steps 1–2) and operational focus (step 3) as complements: neither obsess over strategy nor ignore it. Most entities lack a durable advantage and live or die on disciplined execution, but execution without step 1 risks pouring effort into a structurally hopeless position.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Input: an entity and its competitive environment.
- Output: a strategy stating the entity's structural position, its interaction plan (if advantaged), and a focused operational vision.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Failure: doing the advantage analysis but neglecting execution, or chasing operational excellence inside a structurally doomed position.
- Assumption: the structural position can be honestly assessed; pursuing unrealistic strategic goals reliably produces poor outcomes.
