## When to use
Any conclusion of the form "firm/option X dominates *this* market/segment," or any quantity defined as a residual ("X = whatever's left after the model explains the rest").

## Method
1. **Boundary stress test:** re-draw the scope one level wider and one level narrower. If "dominant" flips to "trivial," the result is an artifact of definitional choice, not structure.
2. Require the boundary to be justified by something *independent of the conclusion* (e.g. where the relevant fixed costs actually stay fixed) — not chosen so the conclusion comes out right.
3. **Residual check:** if the key quantity is defined as what's left over after the model accounts for everything else, that residual could be the posited cause OR measurement error OR luck. Demand an *independent* measurement of the alleged cause, not one backed out from the gap.

## Inputs → Outputs
- In: a scoped dominance claim or a residual-defined quantity.
- Out: whether the conclusion is robust to boundary changes, an independent justification for the chosen boundary (or a flag that it's circular), and whether the residual has any non-circular corroboration.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Some boundaries are natural and well-justified; the skill is to *demand* the justification, not to assume manipulation.
- Re-drawing boundaries can also be abused to dissolve any real finding — anchor the "right" boundary to an external mechanism.
