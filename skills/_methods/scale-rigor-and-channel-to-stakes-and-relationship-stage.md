## When to use
Deciding how much rigor to apply and which channel to use across decisions and relationships of varying stakes and maturity.

## Method
1. Set investigative depth proportional to the downside of being wrong.
2. Choose the channel by trading personal connection against time-per-recipient, sequencing rich-then-lean as trust builds.
3. Front-load high-touch channels early to establish trust, then scale down to leaner channels as the relationship matures.
4. Value standby availability for the counterparty over broadcast volume.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: the decision's stakes; the relationship stage; channel options.
- Outputs: a stakes-matched rigor level and a stage-matched channel choice.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Applying the same depth to trivial and high-stakes decisions.
- Using a lean channel before trust is established.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A practitioner meets a new key source in person to build trust, then shifts to quick messages once the relationship is established.
