# Capitalize expected future liabilities into present wealth

## When to use
When a future cost or needed adjustment is treated as a later problem with no present effect, or a surge appears to grant immunity from a reckoning.

## Method
1. Identify the expected future cost or deferred adjustment.
2. Discount it into present value and subtract it from present wealth.
3. Predict that rational actors change present-period behavior in response to the capitalized liability.
4. Treat a postponement (a counter-cyclical surge that masks the adjustment) as deferral with interest — lengthen the horizon and size for a bigger, later shock.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: a future cost/adjustment; a discount rate. Outputs: the present-wealth reduction and the predicted present-period behavior change.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Requires a defensible discount rate and probability.
- Actors may genuinely ignore the future, muting the present effect.

## Worked example
A looming recapitalization bill is capitalized today, depressing current spending before any loss is booked.
