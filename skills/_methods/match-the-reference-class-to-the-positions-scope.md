## When to use
Evaluating a narrowly-focused position inside a sector whose aggregate/long-run returns look terrible (or deceptively rosy).

## Method
1. Notice that aggregation over an entire sector and its full history averages away the protected local niches (dominant hubs, single regions) where excess returns persist — and can also hide where losses concentrate.
2. Do not let the aggregate determine the prospects of a scope-limited position.
3. Rebuild the reference class from operators comparable in scope to the position under evaluation (e.g. hub-dominant or single-region niche players), and judge against that set.
4. Apply the caution both ways: distrust dismal aggregates for a focused play, and distrust rosy aggregate growth that conceals unprofitable segments.

## Inputs → Outputs
- In: a sector aggregate + a scope-limited position.
- Out: a scope-matched reference class and a corrected expectation for the position.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Requires that scope-matched comparables actually exist and are identifiable.
- The niche's protection must be real; a "pocket of profit" that is itself transient gives a false reference.
