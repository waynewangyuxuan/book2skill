## When to use
Eliciting an estimate or read from a source when you want a clean baseline and want to protect your differentiated view.

## Method
1. Ask "what is the prevailing view?" instead of stating or hinting at your own estimate.
2. Conceal your hypothesis so the answer is not anchored to it and your edge does not leak.
3. Where a source is constrained from a direct answer, engineer the question so the source's reaction to a framing carries the signal.
4. Record the elicited baseline as the reference you will measure your divergence against.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: a source; the question to baseline.
- Outputs: an uncontaminated consensus read; your view kept private.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Anchoring the source by leading with your number.
- Tipping your thesis to a source who can act on or relay it.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
Rather than asking "is 8% growth right?", a practitioner asks "what is the market assuming for growth?" and gets an unanchored baseline.
