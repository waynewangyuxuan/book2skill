# Fade a virtue-vs-vice causal narrative

## When to use
When the favored explanation flatters one party and condemns another, or otherwise feels morally satisfying.

## Method
1. Notice the affective payoff: does adopting this explanation let the speaker feel virtuous, assign blame, or confirm an identity?
2. Treat that payoff as a confound — people adopt morally pleasant or condemnatory stories because they are pleasant, not because they are true.
3. Re-derive the conclusion from mechanism alone, stripping the moral framing.
4. If the mechanism-only derivation no longer supports it, the belief was held for comfort; fade it, expecting it to persist after the mechanism has already flipped.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: a claim plus an honest read of who it flatters/blames. Outputs: a moral-free restatement and whether the claim survives it.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- A morally satisfying claim can still be true; this raises scrutiny, it does not refute.
- Avoid using "it's comforting" as a universal dismissal of claims you dislike.

## Worked example
A "thrifty virtuous saver vs profligate spender" story is faded once the saving is shown to be a forced residual, not a moral choice.
