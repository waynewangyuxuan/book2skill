# Resist monotonic extrapolation of a virtue

## When to use
When a quantity known to be good in scarcity is prescribed in ever-larger doses as if "more is always better."

## Method
1. Establish that the quantity is bounded-optimal, not monotone (too little is bad; so is too much).
2. Locate the system's current position on the diminishing-returns curve.
3. Judge the prescription by stage: value-adding while scarce, value-destroying once saturated.
4. Flag the point where additional doses cross from benefit to harm.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: the quantity; its returns curve; the system's current position. Outputs: a stage-dependent verdict on more-of-X.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- The curve's shape and the current position are estimates.
- Some quantities genuinely are near-monotone over the relevant range.

## Worked example
Investment that drove early growth is shown to have crossed into value destruction once the economy saturated the high-return projects.
