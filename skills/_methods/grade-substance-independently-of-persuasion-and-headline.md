## When to use
Evaluating someone else's argument or research where polished delivery and a confident headline could carry it.

## Method
1. Separate content-quality (the substance) from message-quality (the delivery) as independent axes; each can fail on its own.
2. Treat delivery skill and the loudly-marketed verdict as the least diagnostic parts.
3. Strip the rhetoric and the headline conclusion away.
4. Re-derive the conclusion from the exposed reasoning and evidence alone, and grade the substance on that.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: an argument with its delivery and headline.
- Outputs: a substance grade independent of persuasion, with the failing axis identified.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Crediting a polished, confidently-delivered argument with rigor it lacks.
- Dismissing sound substance because the delivery was poor.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A slickly-presented call collapses once the practitioner strips the rhetoric and finds the underlying reasoning unsupported.
