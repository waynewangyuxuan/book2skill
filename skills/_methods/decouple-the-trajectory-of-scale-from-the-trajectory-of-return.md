## When to use
Reading the history of a growing actor to locate when its underlying advantage began dissolving, or judging whether "still growing" masks structural decline.

## Method
1. Plot two separate trajectories: absolute scale (revenue/size) and per-unit return (margin / return on capital).
2. Do not assume they move together — under unchanged good management, return-on-capital frequently peaks mid-size and erodes through expansion into giant scale.
3. Date the regime change by where margins/returns peaked, not where revenue peaked; that earlier date locates when the advantage began dissolving.
4. Read "the celebrated growth phase" as potentially the advantage-destroying phase.

## Inputs → Outputs
- In: an actor's size history and return history.
- Out: the margin-peak date as the diagnostic moment of advantage erosion, decoupled from the size narrative.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- For genuinely barrier-protected actors in growing markets, scale and return can rise together — don't force the decoupling everywhere.
- Cyclical or one-off margin dips can be mistaken for a regime change; use multi-year averages.
