## When to use
When a previously high-performing organization, team, or system begins to malfunction without an obvious external shock.

## Method
1. Recall that operating structures — culture, incentives, work rules, informality, span of control — are tuned to a particular strategy.
2. Ask whether the strategy has quietly shifted (e.g. from focused-niche to broad-growth, from small to scaled) while the structure stayed the same.
3. If so, diagnose a structure-strategy misfit: the structure that fit the old strategy produces dysfunction under the new one.
4. Resolve it by redesigning the structure for the current strategy — or by questioning whether the strategy change was wise at all (strategy should guide action, not rationalize ambition you already wanted).

## Inputs → Outputs
- Input: an organization whose performance is deteriorating.
- Output: a verdict on whether the cause is structure-strategy misfit, and which to fix (structure or the strategy shift).

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Failure: blaming people/execution when the real cause is a structure left behind by a changed strategy.
- Assumption: the strategy shift is identifiable; sometimes it crept in implicitly via growth pressure.
