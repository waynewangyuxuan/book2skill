## When to use
Delivering any analytical message — a note, a pitch, a verbal brief — to a decision-relevant audience.

## Method
1. Lead with the conclusion; do not bury it.
2. Hook attention and tie the message explicitly to the audience's decision.
3. Force clarity through extreme compression; cut everything non-load-bearing.
4. Acknowledge and address the strongest counter-view.
5. Support with data and keep it understandable.
6. Gate the content against a value checklist before sending: forward-looking, novel, well-researched, transparent, risk-revealing.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: your conclusion, support, counter-views, and audience decision.
- Outputs: a conclusion-first, compressed, decision-tied message.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Building up to the conclusion and losing the audience.
- Backward-looking or already-known content that adds no value.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A note opens with the call and its decision implication, compresses the rest, and pre-empts the main objection — instead of a chronological build-up.
