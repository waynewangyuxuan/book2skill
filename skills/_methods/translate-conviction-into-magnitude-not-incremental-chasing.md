## When to use
When evidence justifies a large change in view and you must decide whether to move fully or incrementally.

## Method
1. When evidence supports a large revision, make the full revision at once.
2. Recognize that breaking a big justified judgment into small repeatedly-confirmed steps often signals risk-aversion in disguise and arrives after the value is gone.
3. Resist manufacturing many decisions to seem productive; make a few high-conviction calls and route surplus energy into maintaining existing ones.
4. Keep the rate of revisions inside a two-sided corridor — too-slow signals stale views, too-fast signals noise-trading.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: the size of the justified revision; your current cadence.
- Outputs: a full-size move (where warranted) and a bounded revision rate.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Comfort-incrementalism that captures the move only after it has happened.
- Over-trading to feel productive.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
Evidence justifies a large change; the practitioner takes the full-size view immediately rather than ratcheting in after the move is mostly over.
