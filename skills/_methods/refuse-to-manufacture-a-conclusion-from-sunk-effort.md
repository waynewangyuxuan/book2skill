## When to use
After investing heavily in data or analysis that turns up no actionable signal.

## Method
1. Pre-commit, before the effort, to accepting a no-signal result.
2. When the analysis shows no clear pattern, conclude "no signal" rather than over-fitting one onto ambiguous data.
3. Do not let the sunk effort bias you toward a manufactured conclusion.
4. Treat the clean null as a valid, valuable output.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: completed analysis; its (absence of) signal; the sunk effort.
- Outputs: an honest "no signal" verdict free of sunk-cost bias.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Inventing a weak pattern to justify the time spent.
- Suppressing the null because it feels like failure.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
After a costly survey yields no usable signal, a practitioner reports the null rather than over-fitting a story to justify the effort.
