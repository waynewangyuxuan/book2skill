# Diagnose conflict risk from real-vs-perceived leverage

## When to use
When assessing escalation risk in a standoff where conventional wisdom assigns the power to one side.

## Method
1. Identify the genuinely scarce resource the standoff turns on.
2. Determine who actually controls it — real leverage.
3. Compare against where consensus places the power — perceived leverage.
4. When perceived and real leverage are mismatched (the side that thinks it is strong holds the weak hand), expect escalation.
5. Check whether the threatened action is actually the opponent's desired outcome, which empties the threat.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: the standoff; the scarce resource; real vs perceived leverage. Outputs: an escalation-risk read and which threats are hollow.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Misjudging the binding scarce resource inverts the conclusion.
- Perceptions can correct, defusing the risk.

## Worked example
A creditor that believes it can dictate terms is shown to hold the weak hand because the debtor controls the scarce demand, so a "we'll stop buying" threat is what the debtor wants anyway.
