# Classify an imbalance by the quality of what funds it

## When to use
When an imbalance (deficit, surplus, asset boom) is judged benign or dangerous on its size alone.

## Method
1. Identify what is funding the imbalance.
2. If the process creating it also creates what later unwinds it (productive investment generating its own future repayment), classify it self-liquidating and benign — rideable.
3. If it is sustained by liability accumulation or an external distortion that does not self-correct, classify it a crisis-in-waiting — position for a snap-back.
4. Split the two cases into opposite trades.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: the imbalance; its funding source. Outputs: self-liquidating vs distortion-sustained verdict and the trade.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Funding quality can shift over the imbalance's life.
- Mixed funding requires weighting the two channels.

## Worked example
A trade deficit funded by productive inbound investment is ridden, while one funded by short-term liability accumulation is positioned for a sharp reversal.
