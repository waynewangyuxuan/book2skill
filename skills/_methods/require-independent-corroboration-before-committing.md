## When to use
Before sizing a decision on a critical claim, particularly one supplied by an interested party.

## Method
1. Flag the claim as load-bearing and identify its source's bias.
2. Treat one source as an interview, not a verified fact.
3. Obtain at least one independent, unconnected corroboration before acting.
4. Scale verification depth to consequence: cheap checks for trivial claims; multiple credible independent sources for high-stakes, irreversible bets. Misallocating rigor is itself a failure.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: a critical claim; its source and bias; the decision's stakes.
- Outputs: a corroborated (or rejected) fact with rigor matched to stakes.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Acting on a single biased principal's word.
- Spending heavy verification on a trivial claim, or skimping on a high-stakes one.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A management claim about demand is held as unconfirmed until a distributor and a customer independently corroborate it before any position is sized.
