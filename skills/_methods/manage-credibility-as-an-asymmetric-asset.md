## When to use
Building and preserving a working relationship with sources, audiences, or counterparties whose trust you will need repeatedly.

## Method
1. Treat trust as an asset that builds slowly and collapses on a single misstep — an asymmetric payoff.
2. Spend early interactions passing the counterparty's competence test and signalling you would detect a lie, sequencing proof before any pitch.
3. Meter every output by whether it builds or spends credibility.
4. Earn trust first; defer the agenda until the credibility balance can fund it.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: a relationship; the counterparty's competence bar.
- Outputs: a sequencing plan that front-loads credibility-building.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Leading with the ask before establishing competence.
- One careless error that wipes out accumulated trust.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A practitioner spends the first meetings demonstrating command of the domain and only later, with trust banked, advances a contrarian view.
