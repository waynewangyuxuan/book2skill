## When to use
Gathering testimony from people whose disclosure is suppressed by the presence of a rival, a superior, or a counterparty in the room.

## Method
1. Map which sources have incentives that conflict or who would penalize candor.
2. Interview each separately, removing the audience that triggers self-censorship.
3. Compare the independent accounts; divergence between them is itself signal.
4. Combine with reframing the question into a low-penalty form so each can disclose safely.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: a roster of sources and their incentive relationships.
- Outputs: per-source candid accounts plus a divergence map.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Collecting a single blended group answer that is uselessly cautious.
- Leaking one source's view to another and contaminating the second account.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
Interviewed together, a manager and a subordinate give identical safe answers; interviewed apart, the subordinate reveals the real constraint.
