## When to use
When you must explain why something succeeded (or failed), several causes are plausible, and the answer should drive a decision.

## Method
1. List the candidate causes of the result.
2. For each cause, write the distinct action it would imply if true (e.g. a transferable competence → replicate it anywhere; a location/context factor → stay put; a structural condition → reproduce that condition elsewhere).
3. Observe that the candidates imply contradictory actions — this is precisely why the diagnosis is decision-relevant, not academic.
4. Gather the evidence that discriminates among them and resolve the cause before acting.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Input: an observed result and its candidate explanations.
- Output: each cause paired with its implied action, and the discriminating evidence needed to choose.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Failure: ranking causes for plausibility without noticing they prescribe opposite actions, then acting on the wrong one.
- Assumption: the causes are genuinely distinguishable by available evidence.
