## When to use
When relying on a visible, committing move to deter rivals — preemptive entry into open territory, a large displayed war chest, an aggressive capacity build.

## Method
1. Before acting, ask: what does this action TELL observers, separate from how it intimidates them?
2. Check for signaling backfire: a first move into unclaimed territory can read as "they must know this market is valuable" and *attract* rivals; a visible war chest can read as a target/provocation rather than a deterrent.
3. Audit lists of recommended "strength signals" for internal contradiction: the same display can deter one rival and provoke another. If the advice prescribes both "show overwhelming force" and "avoid looking threatening," demand the disambiguating condition for which applies when.
4. Choose the move only if the inference observers draw helps you — otherwise the naive "act first / look strong" heuristic inverts.

## Inputs → Outputs
- In: a planned signaling/committing move + the rivals who'll observe it.
- Out: the likely inferences each rival draws, whether they help or hurt, and any contradiction in the surrounding playbook.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Signaling effects depend on what rivals already believe; you're estimating their inference, which is itself uncertain.
- Sometimes the intimidation effect genuinely dominates the information effect — weigh both rather than assuming backfire.
