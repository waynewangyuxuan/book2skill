## When to use
Valuing a cyclical subject where spot earnings and naive multiples are misleading at the cycle extremes.

## Method
1. Recognize that for cyclicals a low multiple often coincides with peak earnings (cycle top) and a high multiple with trough earnings (cycle bottom) — the multiple inverts the cycle signal.
2. Value off normalized through-cycle output rather than spot earnings.
3. Locate where in the cycle the subject currently sits.
4. Do not fight an imminent inflection on the basis of a spot multiple.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: spot earnings and multiple; the through-cycle normalized earnings; cycle position.
- Outputs: a normalized valuation and a cycle-aware read of the multiple.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Buying a cyclical on a "cheap" multiple at peak earnings.
- Mis-estimating the normalized level.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A cyclical looks cheapest on a trailing multiple exactly at peak earnings; normalized through-cycle, it is expensive, and the practitioner avoids the trap.
