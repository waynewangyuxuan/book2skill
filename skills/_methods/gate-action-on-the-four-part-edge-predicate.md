## When to use
Deciding whether a candidate driver, catalyst, or insight deserves research effort and a committed position, before sinking time into it.

## Method
1. Route each candidate through an ordered fail-fast conjunctive gate, cheapest and most decisive tests first.
2. **Relevant / in horizon** — does it land inside your decision horizon? If it resolves past your deadline, it is operationally a failure.
3. **Material** — does its impact exceed a pre-set materiality threshold?
4. **Forecastable by effort** — can diligent research actually predict its trigger, or is it gated by unpredictable exogenous forces?
5. **Not already priced** — is it under-appreciated, or is it already reflected in the reference/price by construction?
6. Discard at the first failure. Only candidates passing all four earn research.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: a list of candidate drivers/catalysts; horizon; materiality threshold; current consensus/price.
- Outputs: a short filtered set of act-worthy drivers, each with the gate it passed.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Treating a useful-but-priced fact as edge; accessibility and visibility are themselves proof of being priced.
- Skipping the horizon gate and acting on a thesis that cannot resolve in time.
- Letting a vivid driver bypass the materiality test.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A widely-reported macro tailwind fails the "not already priced" gate and is dropped despite being real and material; a quietly-shifting input that diligent fieldwork can forecast passes all four and becomes the position.
