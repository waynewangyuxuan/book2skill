## When to use
When a committed position moves against you and you must decide whether to hold, add, or reverse.

## Method
1. Steelman the market's counter-thesis as strongly as you can.
2. Adopt the rule that a counter-thesis fact you cannot disprove should be assumed true.
3. Re-decide the position with the fresh-start question: "would I initiate this today knowing only current info?" — neutralizing sunk cost.
4. Reverse fast when the thesis genuinely breaks; re-examine past failures only when circumstances genuinely change, not from snakebite reflex.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: an adverse-moving position; the counter-thesis; current-only information.
- Outputs: a hold / add / reverse decision freed from anchor and sunk-cost bias.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Defending the original entry instead of re-underwriting from scratch.
- Snakebite avoidance of a sound setup because a prior one failed.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
Asked "would I buy this today?", a practitioner answers no, recognizes the thesis broke, and exits rather than averaging down.
