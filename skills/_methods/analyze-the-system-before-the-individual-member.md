## When to use
Before evaluating any individual member of a larger system (a name within a sector, a unit within a market).

## Method
1. Characterize the containing system first: its cycles, secular forces, and the conditions under which it out- or under-performs.
2. Recognize a member's results are only interpretable in the context of its system; system selection usually explains more variance than within-system skill.
3. Assess the whole-group tide before committing to an individual bet — a strong member can be dragged under by group-level rotation.
4. Only after the system is characterized, drill into the member.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: the member; its containing system's cycles and forces.
- Outputs: a member assessment conditioned on the system tide.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Picking a strong member inside a sinking system.
- Crediting within-system skill for what was system selection.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A standout member underperforms anyway because the whole group rotated out; the system-first analyst had flagged the tide before committing.
