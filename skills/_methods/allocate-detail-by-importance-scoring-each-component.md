## When to use
Allocating finite analytical detail across the many components of a model or write-up.

## Method
1. Score each component on its importance to the conclusion.
2. Scale how much detail and resolution you give each component to that score — elaborate the material, collapse the trivial.
3. Within the work, weight effort toward the forward-looking, value-adding part rather than reconstructing history.
4. Delegate and spot-check the historical reconstruction instead of doing it by hand.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: the component list; an importance score per component.
- Outputs: a detail-allocation plan concentrated on material, forward-looking components.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Lavishing detail on a trivial line because it is easy.
- Over-investing in historical reconstruction at the expense of the forecast.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A practitioner collapses immaterial cost lines to one row and expands the single driver that swings the valuation.
