## When to use
When facing a situation that may carry an ethical conflict affecting your judgment.

## Method
1. Make detecting the dilemma the explicit first step — many conflicts are missed, not mishandled.
2. Classify it: self-serving misconduct vs a structural conflict of interest; each routes to a different remedy.
3. Recognize that disclosure leaves the distorting incentive operative and self-assessed objectivity is unreliable.
4. For a genuine conflict that would bias judgment, recuse and let a disinterested party judge rather than merely disclosing.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: a situation; its conflict type; the available remedies.
- Outputs: a classified dilemma routed to disclosure, recusal, or correction.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Disclosing a conflict and continuing to act, leaving the bias operative.
- Failing to spot the dilemma at all.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
Facing a genuine conflict, a practitioner recuses and hands the call to a disinterested colleague rather than disclosing and proceeding.
