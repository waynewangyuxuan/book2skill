# Price a standard displacement by liquidity depth and cycle phase

## When to use
When a challenger is said to be about to displace an incumbent standard (a reserve currency, a dominant protocol, a default platform).

## Method
1. Discount announced intent and new infrastructure as weak evidence of displacement.
2. Measure the incumbent's real moat: depth of liquidity and willingness to absorb others' excess.
3. Check the challenger against that bar, not against its stated ambition.
4. Locate the current liquidity-cycle phase; new regimes that look ascendant in easy times tend to die when the cycle turns.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: the incumbent and challenger; liquidity-depth and absorption metrics; the cycle phase. Outputs: a displacement-likelihood read.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- A genuine structural shift in absorption willingness can break the incumbent's moat.
- Cycle timing is hard; size for being early.

## Worked example
A would-be reserve-currency challenger is judged unlikely to displace the incumbent because it lacks comparable liquidity depth and its rise coincides with easy-money conditions that will reverse.
