## When to use
Eliciting from a source who is constrained, defensive, or formally barred from answering a direct question.

## Method
1. Sequence questions from comfortable to sensitive so the subject builds confidence and answers the hard ones more fully.
2. Reframe the sensitive question into a symmetric, low-penalty form that lets the source disclose safely.
3. When a direct ask would force a refusal, embed a plausible assertion in the question so the subject reacts to your framing instead of disclosing.
4. Read the reaction (confirm, correct, deflect) as the signal, treating it probabilistically.
5. Keep your own hypothesis concealed throughout to avoid leakage and anchoring.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: a constrained source; a sensitive target question.
- Outputs: a reaction-derived read that bypasses the disclosure barrier.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Leading with the hardest question and triggering shutdown.
- Over-interpreting a reaction the source did not actually intend.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
Barred from stating a number, a source visibly flinches at an embedded over-estimate and relaxes at an under-estimate, bounding the truth.
