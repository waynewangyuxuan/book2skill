## When to use
Deciding how to enter a profitable market dominated by a few incumbents protected by barriers to entry, especially where they already cooperate and a frontal assault would be ruinous.

## Method
1. **Recall the threshold logic:** incumbents attack only when tolerating you costs more than fighting you. Keep your visible threat below that line.
2. **Advance gradually**, in small reversible-looking increments rather than a bold leap; sudden loud commitment triggers retaliation.
3. **Don't attack their core.** Avoid poaching their key customers, talent, suppliers; occupy a segment they reject or undervalue; price at a small fixed discount pegged to theirs so they can't undercut you without self-harm — not a loud low-price war.
4. **Tune your commitment as a signal.** High sunk/fixed cost signals "I'll stay and fight back" (deters their aggression); capped capacity and short cancellable contracts signal "I'm harmless" (invites accommodation). A visible war chest invites war — choose the level by the reaction you want.
5. **Look unique and unrepeatable**, not the first of a wave, so incumbents need not make an example of you to deter imitators.
6. **With multiple incumbents, spread mild damage across all of them** so no single one has cause to respond aggressively. Reframe the whole entry as "let me join the profitable equilibrium," not "I'll take your share."

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: incumbents' cooperation status and barrier strength; available non-overlapping niches; your financing/cost-structure choices; your replicability vs rivals.
- Outputs: a stealth entry posture (pace, scope caps, pricing peg, commitment signal, damage-spreading) that makes peaceful coexistence the incumbents' best response — or a "stay out" decision.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Fails where the market isn't divisible or an incumbent is a single-minded/"messianic" defender who contests any entry regardless of size — then the only sensible move is to stay out (profile the defender first).
- Works only if incumbents see you as unique; if you look like the first of many, they must make an example of you.
- Abandoning the discipline once early success arrives (internal growth pressure) re-crosses the threshold and invites the war you avoided.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A new entrant into a cooperative broadcast oligopoly ran down-market programming the incumbents rejected, financed with heavy debt (signaling it couldn't afford a price war), priced ads at a fixed modest discount, and spread mild competition across all incumbents — so letting it join the club cost each less than fighting it.
