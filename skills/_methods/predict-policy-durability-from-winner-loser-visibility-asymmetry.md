# Predict policy durability from winner-loser visibility asymmetry

## When to use
When predicting whether an ineffective or harmful policy will persist, or diagnosing why a system chronically overshoots.

## Method
1. Map where the policy's benefits and costs land in space and in time.
2. Check for the durability signature: concentrated, near-term, nameable winners plus diffuse, deferred, untraceable losers.
3. If present, predict the policy looks successful and persists regardless of net merit.
4. Use the same geometry to diagnose chronic excess: concentrated-now benefits with dispersed-later costs make individually-rational decisions sum to collective harm.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: the policy; the spatial/temporal incidence of its costs and benefits. Outputs: a durability prediction and an overshoot diagnosis.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- A salient shock can suddenly make diffuse losers visible, breaking durability.
- Incidence mapping requires honest accounting of deferred costs.

## Worked example
A subsidy with visible beneficiaries and invisible diffuse payers is predicted to persist despite a negative net effect.
