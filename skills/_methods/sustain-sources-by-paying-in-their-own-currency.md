## When to use
Maintaining a durable source network whose contributions you need over time.

## Method
1. For each source, identify the currency they actually want — reciprocal insight, status/recognition, or access — established before the first ask.
2. Pay in that currency consistently, not generically.
3. Reinforce every contribution promptly (operant conditioning); reward keeps the channel open.
4. Avoid any dismissal or sign of being out-smarted — a single negative response can extinguish future cooperation permanently.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: a source roster with each one's revealed motivation.
- Outputs: a per-source reciprocity plan and reinforcement habit.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Paying everyone the same way regardless of what they value.
- Letting a contribution go unacknowledged.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
One source is paid in cross-industry insight, another in recognition; both keep contributing because each is reinforced in their own currency.
