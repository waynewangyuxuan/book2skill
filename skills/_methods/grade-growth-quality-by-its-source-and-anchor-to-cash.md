## When to use
Assessing whether reported growth or profit is durable and how much to pay for it.

## Method
1. Compare the output's growth rate to its fundamental driver's growth rate; a metric rising while its core driver is flat is a warning.
2. Split a value change into quantity vs price and extrapolate only the sustainable part.
3. Grade the source of growth: revenue-driven is durable; cost- or asset-driven is finite — discount the multiple you pay accordingly.
4. Use the profit-to-cash ratio as the truth-anchor against accrual spin.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: reported growth; its fundamental driver; price/volume split; profit and cash.
- Outputs: a growth-quality grade and a source-adjusted multiple.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Paying a durable multiple for a one-off cost-cut.
- Trusting accrual earnings that cash does not support.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
Earnings grow while units are flat and cash conversion falls; the practitioner grades the growth as low-quality price/accrual gains and cuts the multiple.
