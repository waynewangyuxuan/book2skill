# Discount post-event explanations built from hindsight flaws

## When to use
After any failure, crisis, or incident, when a tidy story attributes it to flaws the failure itself exposed.

## Method
1. List the flaws cited as causes.
2. For each, ask whether it was identified and treated as load-bearing before the failure, or only made salient by it.
3. Heavily discount flaws visible only in hindsight — every failure exposes flaws that can be retrofitted into a narrative.
4. Require a genuine cause to have been predictively present, not just consistent after the fact.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: a post-mortem and its proposed causes; the pre-event record. Outputs: causes sorted into pre-identified/load-bearing vs hindsight-salient, with the latter down-weighted.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Some real causes are genuinely discovered only post-hoc; pair with an independent mechanism check.
- Guard against the reverse bias of dismissing all post-mortems.

## Worked example
A crisis blamed on a structural flaw is downgraded because the flaw existed for years unremarked and was only labeled fatal once the crisis hit.
