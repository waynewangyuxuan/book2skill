## When to use
You are reading or constructing a causal narrative that attributes a major outcome to intentional, planned action.

## Method
1. Locate the decision the narrative credits.
2. Ask whether, at decision time, the actor treated it as major (resource-heavy, deliberated) or as a minor/tactical choice.
3. If it was minor at the time, downweight the "grand plan produced this" story; many consequential structural choices are mislabeled as minor when made and only look decisive afterward.
4. Adjust the base rate: assume the share of big outcomes that came from deliberate grand strategy is lower than the narrative implies; some are unintended by-products or larger-than-anticipated results.

## Inputs → Outputs
- In: an outcome + its causal narrative.
- Out: a calibrated estimate of how much was intentional design vs accident/hindsight, and reduced confidence in retrospective causal claims.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Over-correction: some big wins genuinely were planned; treating all as luck is its own bias.
- Requires honest reconstruction of contemporaneous framing, which records may not preserve.
- Survivorship bias compounds this: we mostly see the decisions that worked.
