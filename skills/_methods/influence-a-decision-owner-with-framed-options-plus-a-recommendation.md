## When to use
Influencing a decision that someone else formally owns, without authority to make it yourself.

## Method
1. Before the ask, map what is in it for the owner and what they fear; design the ask to satisfy the first and avoid the second.
2. Do the legwork so the owner does not have to.
3. Present a few framed options with your explicit recommendation, rather than a single take-it-or-leave-it.
4. Leave ownership of the choice with them.
5. Time the input before the decision locks, not after.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: the decision; the owner's incentives and fears; framed options.
- Outputs: a recommendation delivered to keep ownership with the decider, timed early.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Presenting one option and triggering resistance.
- Delivering input after the decision is already locked.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A practitioner brings three framed options with a clear recommendation ahead of the deadline; the owner adopts it because the choice still feels theirs.
