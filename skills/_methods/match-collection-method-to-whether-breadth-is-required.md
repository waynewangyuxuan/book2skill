## When to use
Choosing between a broad survey and a few deep expert interviews to answer a question.

## Method
1. Ask whether the question truly needs the aggregate of many respondents or whether a few experts suffice; reserve the costly broad effort for genuine aggregate questions.
2. Design every elicitation question to yield a number or scaled rating, not a category or yes/no, so answers aggregate into distributions and compare across repeated waves.
3. Validate the instrument before fielding: run draft questions against mock answers and attempt the intended analysis; if it does not yield what you need, revise.
4. Pre-commit to accepting a no-signal result despite the sunk effort.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: the question; whether it is aggregate or expert-shaped; a draft instrument.
- Outputs: the right collection method plus a validated, quantitative, longitudinally-comparable instrument.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Running an expensive survey for a question a few experts could answer.
- Fielding an instrument whose answers cannot be aggregated or compared across waves.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A practitioner mock-tests a draft questionnaire, finds the free-text answers un-aggregable, and rewrites them as 1–5 scales before fielding.
