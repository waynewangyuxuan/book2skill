# Score a recurring proposal by periodicity and hit-rate

## When to use
When a proposal, reform, or prediction that has appeared before resurfaces and is being treated as fresh.

## Method
1. Trace how often the proposal has recurred across history.
2. Tally how often it has ever actually succeeded.
3. A high recurrence with a near-zero hit-rate carries a strong prior of futility.
4. Read the recurrence itself as a signal that the underlying driver has never been addressed.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: the proposal; its recurrence history; its success record. Outputs: a futility prior plus the implied unaddressed driver.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Conditions may finally have changed, making this instance different — require the change to be named.
- Survivorship in the historical record can hide quiet successes.

## Worked example
A periodically-revived reform that has never resolved its target imbalance gets a high futility prior and points to a driver no version has touched.
