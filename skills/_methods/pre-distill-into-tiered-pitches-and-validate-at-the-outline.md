## When to use
Preparing a message you will deliver under uncertain time and attention, and structuring a larger artifact.

## Method
1. Prepare tiered versions — an ultra-short pitch and a slightly longer one; deliver the short first and expand only if the audience asks.
2. Match information density to demand: when pushing your point keep each unit sparse; when the audience pulls and may probe anything, prepare dense comprehensive backup.
3. Draft and get feedback on an outline before building; restructuring an outline costs a fraction of restructuring a finished artifact.
4. For a message delivered many times, refine it via the questions you get between deliveries so the final delivery is tightest.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: the message; the delivery mode (push/pull); an outline.
- Outputs: tiered pitches, density matched to mode, and an outline-validated structure.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Delivering the long version when the audience wanted the headline.
- Building the full artifact before validating its structure.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A practitioner gets outline feedback that reorders the argument, saving a costly rewrite of the finished note.
