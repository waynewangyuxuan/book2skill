## When to use
Making a case to a skeptical audience, or stress-testing whether your own analysis is complete.

## Method
1. Articulate the strongest version of the opposing argument (steelman it).
2. If you cannot state the other side well, treat that as evidence your analysis is unfinished, and complete it.
3. Build the opposing case into your own message and refute it point by point.
4. Anchor all criticism to facts, not personalities, to preserve repeated-game access; in dialogue, start from the other party's position.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: your case; the strongest opposing case.
- Outputs: a message that pre-empts and refutes the counter-case, fact-anchored.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Attacking a weak strawman of the opposing view.
- Personalizing criticism and burning future access.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A practitioner who cannot articulate the bear case realizes the analysis is incomplete, builds it, refutes it, and only then publishes.
