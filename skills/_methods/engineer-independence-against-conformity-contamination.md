## When to use
Forming an independent view in an environment saturated with consensus, and managing the entry-anchor bias afterward.

## Method
1. Recognize herd influence alters perception itself, not just expressed behavior.
2. Commit your view before exposure to the consensus firehose, then update deliberately.
3. Weigh physical/informational distance from the consensus stream as an asset, not a handicap.
4. Account for loss aversion (a loss hurts more than an equal gain pleases) by evaluating positions on forward expected value, not on the entry reference point.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: your pre-exposure view; the consensus stream; entry anchors.
- Outputs: an independence-protected view evaluated on forward EV.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Reading consensus first and unconsciously adopting it as your own.
- Holding a loser because of the entry anchor.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A practitioner records an estimate before reading the consensus, then finds their independent number was anchored away once exposed — confirming the value of committing first.
