## When to use
When a framework promotes a punchy, counterintuitive rule ("growth is the enemy," "X dominates everything, ignore the rest") and starts applying it broadly.

## Method
1. State the maxim precisely, then ask: under what conditions was it derived?
2. Identify the mechanism that makes it true and trace where that mechanism is present vs absent.
3. Find the in-framework cases the maxim does **not** cover (often the author's own typology limits it).
4. Treat any "one variable dominates, ignore the rest" claim as an empirical bet about dominance-ordering, not a theorem; look for a case where an "ignored" factor flips the outcome.
5. Rewrite the maxim with its scope condition attached, and refuse to apply it outside that scope.

## Inputs → Outputs
- In: a general maxim + the cases it's being applied to.
- Out: the scoped version ("true when ___, false/neutral when ___") and a flag on any current out-of-scope application.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- The maxim may genuinely be broad — don't reflexively shrink it; find the actual boundary by mechanism, not taste.
- Beware the opposite error: dismissing a true general principle because you found one edge case. Weigh how often the scope condition holds.
