## When to use
Choosing where to hunt for signal when you need a differentiated read, not table-stakes coverage.

## Method
1. Treat any widely-used or published channel as already reflected in the reference by construction.
2. Hunt up and down the value chain and through less-trafficked, non-insider feeders and person-to-person channels.
3. Weight a source's edge value inversely to its popularity.
4. If your supporting evidence was cheap and accessible, presume it is priced in and keep looking.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: candidate channels ranked by traffic/popularity.
- Outputs: a routing toward low-traffic, high-edge channels.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Mistaking convenient public data for edge.
- Over-romanticizing obscure sources that are obscure because they are unreliable.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
Instead of the headline data everyone watches, a practitioner cultivates a small upstream supplier whose order book leads the published numbers.
