## When to use
Triaging necessary-but-low-differentiation tasks that consume time without creating edge.

## Method
1. Sort necessary work by whether it builds your unique value or is generic.
2. Route generic work off your plate via delegate / automate / outsource.
3. Keep in-house only what is genuinely proprietary or needs fast turnaround you control.
4. Guard quality on routed work with spot-checks and self-checks, not by re-doing it — re-doing destroys the leverage.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: task inventory tagged proprietary vs generic; turnaround needs.
- Outputs: a routing plan plus a spot-check protocol.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Re-doing delegated work, erasing the time savings.
- Outsourcing something secretly load-bearing for edge.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A practitioner outsources routine data assembly and spot-checks a sample, freeing time for proprietary fieldwork.
