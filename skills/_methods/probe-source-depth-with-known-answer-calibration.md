## When to use
Vetting an unfamiliar source's competence and honesty before relying on their non-public claims.

## Method
1. Prepare questions whose answers you already hold and that are uncontroversial.
2. Ask them early; gauge the source's accuracy and depth against the known answers.
3. Let the source see you already hold non-public knowledge, signalling you would detect a lie — this raises their cost of embellishment.
4. Only after calibration, weight their answers to questions you cannot independently verify.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: a new source; a set of known-answer probe questions.
- Outputs: a competence/honesty calibration that scales how much you trust their novel claims.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Trusting fluent confidence without calibration.
- Making the source feel tested in a way that closes the channel — keep it conversational.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A practitioner opens with two facts they already know; the source nails one and fumbles the other, calibrating how much weight to give the rest.
