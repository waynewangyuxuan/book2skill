## When to use
Operating in a market where the same behavioral biases affect both you and the crowd.

## Method
1. Catalogue the predictable behavioral errors that recur in the domain.
2. On your own side, build mechanical routines to remove each error from your process.
3. On the crowd's side, treat the same errors as an exploitable, recurring edge — position to benefit when others commit them.
4. Keep the two postures separate so removing your own bias does not blind you to the crowd's.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: a catalogue of recurring biases; your process; crowd behavior.
- Outputs: a self-debiasing protocol plus a bias-exploitation strategy.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Removing your own bias but ignoring the tradeable edge in others'.
- Assuming you are immune while trading others' errors.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A practitioner removes anchoring from their own revisions while positioning to profit from the crowd's slow, anchored adjustment to the same news.
