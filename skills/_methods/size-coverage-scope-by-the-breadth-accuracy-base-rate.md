## When to use
Deciding how many items (names, beats, problems) to actively cover at once.

## Method
1. Treat the count of items under active coverage as a capacity variable that is inversely related to per-item accuracy.
2. Set the cap at an empirically grounded depth threshold — the most you can keep a genuine edge on — calibrated against peers, not against ambition.
3. Before expanding, recognize that adding items degrades quality across all of them, not just the marginal one.
4. Reduce effective workload by collapsing co-driven items (see correlation-based scope sizing) so capacity goes to truly independent bets.

## Inputs → Outputs
- Inputs: candidate coverage list; your demonstrated per-item depth; peer benchmarks.
- Outputs: a capped, defensible coverage scope.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Equating nominal breadth with productivity; breadth past the threshold is negative-yield.
- Ignoring correlation structure and over-counting independent work.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
A practitioner cuts an over-ambitious list to a focused set; measured accuracy per item rises even as total items fall.
