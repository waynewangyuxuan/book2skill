## When to use
When an interested party offers an explanation for an outcome that happens to absolve them.

## Method
1. Notice when the offered cause is both benign and exculpatory for the explainer.
2. Treat such a convenient explanation as motivated until tested.
3. Construct a disinterested counterfactual: what would a neutral analyst conclude, and does the explanation survive?
4. Require the explanation to clear the counterfactual before crediting it.

## Inputs to Outputs
- Inputs: an outcome; an interested party's exculpatory explanation; a neutral counterfactual.
- Outputs: a tested verdict on whether the benign explanation holds.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Accepting a self-clearing story because it is structurally plausible.
- Over-suspecting an explanation that is both convenient and true.

## Worked example (illustrative, single)
An insider blames an industry-wide factor that also clears them of a conflict; the practitioner tests it against peers who lacked the conflict and finds the story does not hold.
