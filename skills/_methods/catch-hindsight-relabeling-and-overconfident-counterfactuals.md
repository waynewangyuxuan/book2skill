## When to use
When a narrative (a) converts a botched move into a success after the result is known, or (b) renders confident verdicts on roads not taken ("there was nothing else they could have done").

## Method
1. **Hindsight test:** would the actor, *before* the outcome, have described this as the plan? If not, the "strategy" is retrofitted, and the case can't teach what good strategy looks like — a botched move and a planned one become indistinguishable once you're free to relabel intent.
2. **Counterfactual test:** single-history what-ifs are not observable. Ask whether there's *any* alternative action whose outcome genuinely can't be ruled out. If so, a categorical "no other option" overstates the evidence — and conveniently protects the framework's own prescription.
3. Separate "this is what happened" (observable) from "this is why, and nothing else was possible" (inference dressed as fact).

## Inputs → Outputs
- In: an after-the-fact explanation or counterfactual verdict.
- Out: whether the lesson is genuinely supported or is hindsight/retrofit, and which alternatives were dismissed without evidence.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Some counterfactuals are well-constrained by strong priors; don't treat all as empty — weigh the prior.
- Retrofitting is sometimes honest re-interpretation; the tell is whether intent was stated *before* the result.
