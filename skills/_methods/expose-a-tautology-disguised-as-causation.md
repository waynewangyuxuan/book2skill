# Expose a tautology disguised as causation

## When to use
On any "X is caused by Y" claim where X and Y might be two names for the same accounting quantity.

## Method
1. Check whether X equals Y (or a component of Y) by definition.
2. If yes, label the claim a tautology with no causal content.
3. Redirect to the real causal question: what set the components the identity ties together?

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: a causal claim; the relevant identities. Outputs: verdict (causal vs tautological) plus the relocated causal question.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- A relationship can be both an identity and have a genuine causal driver behind its components; don't drop the deeper question after flagging the tautology.

## Worked example
"High investment caused the high saving rate" is exposed as a restatement of an identity, redirecting attention to what forced the components apart.
