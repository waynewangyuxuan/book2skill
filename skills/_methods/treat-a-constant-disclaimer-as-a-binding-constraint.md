## When to use
When a method's prescribed moves repeatedly require a disclaimer ("...without breaking the law," "...without crossing the ethical line," "...as long as you stay compliant"), especially when the most powerful version of the move is exactly the one nearest the line.

## Method
1. Notice the recurring disclaimer and name the line it's protecting (legal, ethical, regulatory).
2. Treat that line as an external binding constraint, not a footnote — it may remove precisely the high-value region of the method's action space.
3. **Operational-test check:** can two reasonable practitioners agree, *ex ante*, which side of the line a specific proposed action falls on? If not, the method has offloaded its hardest judgment onto the user with no usable rule.
4. Re-evaluate the method's value *restricted to the clearly-permissible region*. If little power remains there, the method is far weaker in practice than on paper.

## Inputs → Outputs
- In: a method whose moves carry a recurring boundary disclaimer.
- Out: the binding constraint made explicit, whether the permissible region is operationally well-defined, and the method's realistic (constrained) usefulness.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- Some lines are clear and easily respected; the skill triggers when the *powerful* moves cluster against the line and the line is fuzzy.
- This evaluates deployability, not truth — a method can be correct yet unusable; keep the two judgments separate.
