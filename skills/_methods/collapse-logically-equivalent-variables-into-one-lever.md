# Collapse logically-equivalent variables into one lever

## When to use
When a debate treats two quantities as independent levers although an identity forces them equal, or when many distinct-looking instruments all act through one underlying ratio.

## Method
1. Establish the identity linking the quantities.
2. Treat them as one object: any intervention on one is an equal-sized intervention on the other.
3. Trace each candidate instrument through the intermediate ratio it really moves; instruments that move the same ratio are equivalent, whatever their labels.
4. Use this to reclassify "different" policies as one policy, and to find hidden members of the same lever family.

## Inputs -> Outputs
Inputs: the identity; the instrument set. Outputs: a single collapsed lever plus the roster of instruments that operate it.

## Failure modes / assumptions
- The identity must be exact for the collapse to hold.
- Second-order channels can differ even when the first-order lever is shared.

## Worked example
Several differently-named measures are shown to all shift one relative-price ratio, so they are analyzed as a single lever rather than independent tools.
