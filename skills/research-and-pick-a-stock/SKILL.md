# research-and-pick-a-stock

## Spine
Equity research is an assembly line whose only product is a *differentiated, tradeable* stock call: ruthlessly concentrate every hour of qualitative and quantitative effort on the few "critical factors" where your view can plausibly diverge from consensus, forecast those factors under uncertainty, convert the forecast into a price target, and only act when your edge is material, forecastable-by-you, in-horizon, AND not-yet-priced — then communicate it so others move.

## Procedure

### S1 — Set the attention frontier (scoping gate)
**Goal:** Decide where in your universe effort can produce alpha, before doing any analysis. Concentrate work where your view can diverge from consensus rather than spreading evenly (*"concentrate effort at the divergence frontier"*, historian-001 / trader-001). Size how many names/sub-topics you can cover by measuring the universe's internal homogeneity/correlation — a tightly-correlated set lets one deep view cover many names (*"size scope by the breadth-accuracy base rate"*, historian-003; *"estimate effective scope from correlation structure"*, historian-004). Filter every candidate activity through one primary objective: picking stocks, not becoming a company encyclopedia (*"filter all activity through a single primary objective"*, teacher-008).
**Gate:** If you cannot name *where* a future divergence from consensus would come from, the name is coverage, not a call — park it and move on.

### S2 — Protect offense; route the rest (organization gate)
**Goal:** Defend the deep-work blocks that generate edge ("offense") from commoditized maintenance ("defense"). Partition all work into offense vs defense by competitor-overlap and shield offense (*"classify activity as offense vs defense by competitor-overlap"*, historian-006; *"partition work into offense and defense, protect offense"*, teacher-009). Batch interruptions and price them at their true switching cost (*"price interruptions at their true switching cost and batch inputs"*, historian-007). Delegate/automate/outsource required-but-low-value tasks (*"triage required-but-low-value tasks via delegate/automate/outsource"*, teacher-011).
**Gate:** If >X% of the week is defense, you have no time for edge — restructure before continuing.

### S3 — Find the critical factors (qualitative core)
**Goal:** Identify the handful of variables that actually drive the stock, top-down. Diagnose the sector/system before the individual company (*"diagnose the system before the unit — top-down before bottom-up"*, historian-017; *"analyze the system before any individual member"*, teacher-025). Work backward from the stock outcome to its observable root drivers (*"decompose backward from outcome to observable root drivers"*, teacher-026). Qualify each candidate driver through a conjunctive filter — it must be **material AND have a forecastable catalyst** (*"qualify a driver by a conjunctive materiality-plus-catalyst filter"*, historian-021; *"prefer factors whose triggers are forecastable through effort"*, teacher-034); then keep only a few, ranked on impact × forecastability (*"rank candidates on impact and forecastability, keep only a few"*, teacher-035). Surface non-obvious drivers by studying the extremes, not the average (*"surface drivers by studying the extremes, not the average"*, teacher-032).
**Gate:** A driver that is important but its trigger is *not* forecastable by your own effort (e.g., a macro variable) is a risk to monitor, not a critical factor to research — exclude it.

### S4 — Build proprietary sources and elicit the baseline (insight-sourcing gate)
**Goal:** Acquire information edge on the critical factors from uncrowded channels without contaminating it. Prefer uncrowded, non-public, independent channels over consensus feeds (*"prefer uncrowded, non-public channels for edge"*, historian-014; *"source edge from under-watched adjacencies and independents"*, trader-007). Elicit the market/source baseline *neutrally*, before revealing your own view, to avoid leaking and self-exposure (*"elicit the baseline neutrally to avoid contamination and self-exposure"*, teacher-033 / trader-008). Predict each source's distortion from its incentives and provenance, and require independent corroboration before relying on it (*"predict a source's distortion from its incentives and provenance"*, historian-012; *"require independent corroboration before committing"*, historian-024). Read honesty from the least-controllable channels and treat cues as hypotheses, not proof (*"weight the least-controllable channels when reading honesty"*, teacher-044 / trader-012). Sustain access by paying sources in their own currency (*"sustain sources by paying in their own currency"*, teacher-048).
**Gate:** Branch — if a source's incentive would distort the very factor you need, isolate it and seek a structurally independent corroborator before using it; uncorroborated single-source claims do not enter the forecast.

### S5 — Forecast the critical factors quantitatively (quantitative core)
**Goal:** Convert qualitative insight into numeric forecasts of the critical factors, with uncertainty. Recast all reported data into your own neutral template before comparing, and defend against window/denominator manipulation (*"recast reported results into your own neutral format before comparing"*, teacher-051; *"enforce measurement-window hygiene"*, quant-035). Make history-continuity the null and force evidence for any inflection (*"make history-continuity the null and force evidence for any inflection"*, quant-020). Use forensic accounting to hunt anomalies on extreme-outcome windows and grade earnings quality by source vs cash (*"common-size and screen for anomalies on extreme-outcome windows"*, quant-022; *"grade profit durability by source and anchor to cash"*, trader-020). Where you model statistically: explore visually before fitting, gate correlation behind a causal mechanism, and **validate the model out-of-sample / blind temporal replay** (*"gate model-fitting behind visual exploratory analysis"*, quant-013; *"gate correlation behind a causal-mechanism test"*, historian-029; *"backtest a forecasting model out-of-sample"*, quant-017 / *"validate models by blind temporal replay; weight effort forward"*, historian-030). Express each target as a probability-weighted range with history-bounded tails (*"frame a target as a probability-weighted range with history-bounded tails and an asymmetry hurdle"*, quant-034). Separate model parameters from logic and self-audit via redundant cross-computation (*"separate parameters from logic and preserve assumption history"*, quant-028; *"verify a model with redundant cross-computation"*, quant-027).
**Gate:** If a model fails out-of-sample replay, or a correlation has no causal mechanism, do not let it drive the target — it is decoration. Refuse to manufacture a conclusion from sunk modeling effort (*"refuse to manufacture a conclusion from sunk effort"*, teacher-065).

### S6 — Stress the forecast with scenarios (debiasing gate)
**Goal:** Force breadth onto your own forecast to counter confirmation bias. Construct deliberately extreme dual-tail scenarios as a debiasing tool, varying only the highest-leverage drivers (*"pre-empt confirmation bias with forced dual-tail scenarios"*, historian-035; *"vary only the highest-leverage drivers across scenarios"*, teacher-080; *"construct scenarios as a debiasing tool and invest precision in the tails"*, trader-021). Bound scenarios to history-anchored plausibility and check assumptions for joint feasibility against the domain's trade-offs (*"bound scenarios to history-anchored plausibility and weight them"*, teacher-082; *"check scenario assumptions for joint feasibility against trade-offs"*, quant-033).
**Gate:** If your "base case" is also your "hoped-for case," you have not stress-tested — rebuild the downside tail until it is genuinely uncomfortable.

### S7 — Value and decide the call (valuation + edge gate)
**Goal:** Turn the forecast into a price target and a buy/sell/hold decision. Value using the audience's actual evaluation method (usually single-period multiples), cross-validated by a structurally independent second method (*"use the audience's evaluation method, not the theoretically best one"*, teacher-084; *"cross-validate with a structurally independent second method"*, teacher-085); guard against reverse-engineering a flexible method to a foregone answer (*"guard against reverse-engineering a flexible method to a foregone answer"*, teacher-086). Decompose the implied move into expectations-shift vs realized substance to locate where you actually diverge (*"decompose a change into expectation-shift vs realized-result"*, historian-019). **THE central gate:** admit the call only if it passes the four-part edge predicate — material AND in-horizon AND forecastable-by-you AND not-yet-priced (*"gate action on the four-part edge predicate"*, trader-002 / quant-031). Require an edge on at least one orthogonal dimension — financial forecast, valuation, or sentiment (*"require an edge on at least one orthogonal dimension before acting"*, quant-032). Default to being wrong when you diverge from a strong crowd (*"default to being wrong when you diverge from a strong crowd"*, teacher-088).
**Gate (hard stop):** Right-but-priced, or mispriced-but-unforecastable/out-of-horizon → **no trade**. This is the most common way to be right and make no money.

### S8 — Require a catalyst and define convergence (timing gate)
**Goal:** Ensure your differentiated view will become the market's view inside your horizon. Require a high-quality, pre-registered catalyst before acting and demand an explicit time-to-convergence, not just a valuation gap (*"require a high-quality catalyst before acting on a correct view"*, teacher-089; *"require time-to-convergence, not just a valuation gap"*, trader-023). Read crowd saturation / sentiment extremes and ownership-style support to time the turn (*"read sentiment extremes and overextension as reversal signals"*, historian-038; *"read crowd saturation and ownership-style support to time turns"*, trader-025); optionally time entry with the price/volume tape while selecting on fundamentals (*"select on fundamentals, time entry with the price/volume tape"*, trader-034).
**Gate:** Correct view + no identifiable catalyst within horizon → hold off; a thesis with no convergence mechanism is a value trap. Judge the call on horizon-bounded timing, not just direction (*"judge a call on horizon-bounded timing, not just direction"*, historian-037).

### S9 — Size, pre-register exits, and manage the position (discipline gate)
**Goal:** Translate conviction into position size and pre-commit the decisions that bias will later corrupt. Scale conviction to information completeness and translate it into magnitude, not incremental chasing (*"scale conviction to information completeness"*, quant-005; *"translate conviction into magnitude, not incremental chasing"*, trader-030). Gate sizing on a payoff-asymmetry hurdle (*"gate sizing on a payoff-asymmetry hurdle"*, trader-027). Pre-register falsifiable triggers and mechanical exit rules *before* acting, and automate what bias corrupts (*"pre-register falsifiable triggers and mechanical exit rules"*, quant-041 / *"pre-commit exit thresholds before acting, to block later bias"*, teacher-092). Document reasoning contemporaneously to defeat hindsight (*"document reasoning contemporaneously to defeat hindsight revision"*, teacher-101). Make self-bias visible with mechanical instruments, not willpower, and run a fixed pre-decision questionnaire including a pre-mortem (*"make self-bias visible with mechanical instruments, not willpower"*, skeptic-013; *"run a fixed pre-decision questionnaire including a pre-mortem"*, teacher-091).
**Gate:** No position goes on without pre-registered exits.

### S10 — Communicate so others act (delivery gate)
**Goal:** Get the call into others' decisions. Diagnose content-quality and message-quality separately, then lead with the conclusion (*"diagnose communication on content vs message and lead with the conclusion"*, historian-045). Gate the content against a value checklist before communicating, scale rigor/channel to stakes and audience, and front-load high-touch channels (*"gate content against a value checklist before communicating"*, teacher-105; *"scale rigor and channel choice to stakes, audience, and relationship stage"*, historian-046; *"choose the channel by connection-versus-time, front-loading high-touch"*, teacher-108). Pre-distill into tiered pitches, steelman and preemptively refute the strongest opposing view, attacking ideas not people (*"pre-distill into tiered pitches and expand only on demand"*, teacher-110; *"persuade by steelmanning the counter-case and attacking ideas not people"*, historian-047). Research what the audience values and master their vocabulary (*"research what your audience actually values, then build to it"*, teacher-117).
**Gate:** Validate structure at the outline stage before building the full note (*"validate structure at the outline stage before building"*, teacher-113).

### S11 — Re-underwrite, learn, and act ethically (cross-cutting loop)
**Goal:** Keep the thesis honest over time and decide cleanly under conflict. Periodically re-decide the held thesis from scratch and reset sunk-cost/anchor reflexes (*"periodically re-decide a held thesis as if from scratch"*, teacher-093; *"reframe decisions off the entry anchor to neutralize asymmetric loss"*, quant-046). Size revisions to the evidence not to comfort, re-underwrite fast on validated disconfirmation, and reverse the default on disconfirming evidence and self-attribution (*"size a revision to the evidence, not to comfort"*, quant-043; *"re-underwrite under loss and reverse fast on validated disconfirmation"*, trader-029; *"reverse the default on disconfirming evidence and self-attribution"*, skeptic-016). For ethics: detect the dilemma first, apply outsider-explainability as a fast screen, recuse rather than disclose when an incentive would bias judgment, and lock in the hard-right choice with speed and irreversibility (*"detect the ethical dilemma first, then classify its type"*, teacher-119; *"apply outsider-explainability as a fast ethics screen"*, teacher-120; *"recuse rather than disclose when an incentive would bias judgment"*, skeptic-017; *"resolve ethics conflicts via external frames and irreversible commitment"*, historian-048).
**Loop / edges:** This step feeds back. On validated disconfirmation → loop to S5/S7 (re-forecast, re-decide). On thesis confirmed but un-converged at horizon → loop to S8 (re-assess catalyst/timing). S6 (scenario debiasing) and S11 (re-underwriting) run in parallel against your own confirmation bias throughout. The ethics screen can fire at any step and override it (recuse → abandon the call regardless of edge).

## When this whole procedure misleads (guardrails)
- **Edge is presumed where there is none.** The spine assumes a crowded, semi-efficient market where differentiated, forecastable, unpriced views exist and converge via catalysts. In a genuinely efficient name, or a reflexive/manipulated market where price drives fundamentals, the four-part predicate (S7) will keep rejecting — and forcing a call anyway manufactures false edge. The honest output is often "no trade," which this procedure must be allowed to return.
- **Critical-factor tunnel vision.** Concentrating on a few pre-identified critical factors (S3) is the engine, but it structurally blinds you to a *new* factor that becomes dominant outside your frame (regime change, exogenous shock). The procedure has no built-in scan for "what factor did I not list?" — re-run S3 from scratch periodically, not just S5–S7.
- **Process-quality ≠ outcome.** The gates make a call *defensible and disciplined*, not *correct*. A rigorously-built, well-communicated thesis can still be wrong; the asymmetry hurdle (S7/S9) and pre-registered exits (S9) are what protect capital when it is — do not let process rigor substitute for being humble about the base rate of being right.
- **Lost-Exhibits caveat (source-specific).** The book's quantitative steps lean on numbered Exhibits (models, tables, charts) absent from the source text; treat S5/S7 quantitative mechanics as method-shapes, not as a parameter recipe — do not fabricate specific thresholds, multiples, or coefficients.
